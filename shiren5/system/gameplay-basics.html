Mechanics

Turns

Turns and Turn-Ending Actions

Being a turn-based roguelike, Shiren and monsters take turns when performing actions.
Generally, turn-ending actions include movement, attacks, using an item, and stepping in place.
Your turn does not end from idling, checking item details, or otherwise navigating menus.

The following actions are easy to forget / mistake:

  • Equipping a weapon, shield, or bracelet does end the turn, but equipping a projectile or staff does not.
  • Talking to an ally character or NPC does end the turn.
  • Setting a trap on the ground does not end the turn.

Turns Elapsing

Various effects and events occur based on the number of turns that have elapsed.
Do your best to avoid wasting turns with meaningless actions.

  • Fullness decreases by 1 every 10 turns.
  • Items that apply status conditions generally last a set number of turns.
  • A new monster is generated every N turns. (N depends on the dungeon)
  • A new enemy Shopkeep is generated every 5 turns when stealing from shops.
  • Time of day changes between day and night. (Some dungeons are daytime-only)
  • The Wind of Kron blows you out of the dungeon if you remain on one floor too long. (See next section)
  • HP regenerates every turn at a rate dependent on your max HP.
    Examples:
    15~50 = 1, 2, 1, 2...
    51~66 = 1, 1, 1, 2...
    96~101 = 1, 1, 1, 1...
    124 = 1, 1, 1, 0...
    200+ = 1, 0, 1, 0...

Wind of Kron

If you remain on the same floor of a dungeon for a long time, a strong wind begins to blow.
The number of turns depends on the dungeon, but you'll have 3 warnings before being blown away.
You'll automatically fail the adventure and lose all items and Gitan if that happens.
If you can't make it to the exit in time, read an Escape Scroll or eat Undo Grass.

In-game messages:

  1. You feel the wind picking up.
  2. The wind blows harder.
  3. The wind gusts vigorously!
  4. (Player) was blown away by a gust!

Day / Night

Many dungeons switch between day and night based on the number of elapsed turns.
Day and night have their own monster tables, and all previous time of day monsters vanish
and the floor is populated with new monsters when time of day changes.
The background music changes based on time of day as well.

Day

  • Direct attacks deal damage to monsters normally.
  • Can't use abilities during the day.
  • Monsters will level up to their next rank when applicable.
    (example: Mamel → Pit Mamel)

Night

  • Direct attacks only deal 1 damage to night monsters.
  • Fixed damage attacks like Dragon Grass also only deal 1 damage.
    (However, special effects from staves, scrolls, etc. still work)
  • Primarily use abilities to deal damage.
  • Field of View changes to 1-tile radius without a torch equipped.
    (You also can't read scrolls and take more damage from night monsters without light)
  • Night monsters have unpredictable movement, and will attack any adjacent creature.
  • Night monsters will limit break when they level up instead of moving to their next rank.
    (example: Dark Mamel → Dark Mamel2)
  • Can't use the Scout command unless you read a Navigation Scroll or Fixer Scroll.
  • Items dropped by night monsters are always blessed, and are selected from the dungeon's shop table.

Auras

Monsters sometimes have auras when they're generated naturally, including initial floor population.
They'll never have auras if they weren't generated in a natural manner, such as being summoned.
Whether or not monsters can generate with auras depends on the dungeon and specific floor.

Defeating monsters with auras grants double experience points, so take advantage of that when possible.
Auras can be removed by reading a Dispel Aura Scroll, swinging a Seal Staff, or throwing Talismans.
There are three types of auras: Red, Blue, and Yellow.

  • Red: Doubles attack power.
  • Blue: Doubles defense.
  • Yellow: Increases action speed by 2 stages.

Super Shiren

There's a chance for one of the following to occur when you defeat enemies based on Shiren's status:

Normal

  • Chance to gain Awakened-ish status.

Awakened-ish

  • Chance to gain Super status.

Super (gain a bonus every 10 enemies you defeat)

  • Gain Overwhelming status - Increases attack power. (3 floors)
  • Gain Indomitable status - Increases defense. (3 floors)
  • Gain Super Swift status - Increases action speed. (50 turns / 100 turns from Shiren's perspective)
  • Gain Modder status - Earn x4.0 equipment exp from defeating monsters. (2 floors)
  • Gain Fast Striking status - Attack twice per turn. (50 turns)
  • Gain Precise status - All attacks become critical hits. (50 turns)
  • ※ Overwhelming and Indomitable can be repeated to boost attack or defense multiple times.

In addition, your direct attacks can randomly inflict Asleep, Confused, Blind, Paralyzed, Sealed,
Berserk, or Shadow Bound status while you have Super status. (See Status Conditions for details)

Shiren's Awakened-ish and Super statuses can be lost by the following events:

  • Become afflicted by a negative status condition.
  • Step on a trap.
  • When a remaining fullness warning message gets displayed.
    (1st: 20 fullness, 2nd: 10 fullness)
  • Fullness reaches 0.
  • Your HP falls below 30% of your max HP.
    (Indicated by Danger status and a red UI)
  • ※ The 0 fullness and less than 30% HP checks occur every turn, so try to recover from them right away.

Equipment Growth

Defeating monsters with direct attacks gradually upgrades your equipped weapon and shield.

  • Each monster has a set number of equipment experience points (skill points), separate from visible exp.
    (example: Pit Mamel is worth 2 skill points)
  • Equipment will level up once you pass the assigned skill point threshold for that weapon or shield.
    (example: Ordinary Stick levels up after you earn 50 skill points)
  • Generally, higher level monsters offer more skill points.

See the Tips and Tricks page for efficient equipment leveling tips.

Synthesis

Mechanic where you combine items to merge abilities, upgrade value, and use counts.

Synthesis Pot

  • Lets you combine weapons with weapons, shields with shields, and staves with staves.
  • The first weapon or shield acts as a base, and upgrade value and runes of subsequent items transfer to it.
    (example: Plain Targe+1 followed by Counter Shield+3 → Plain Targe+4 with the Retribution rune)
  • Staves must have the same name, and the number of uses will be added together.
    (Useful to synthesize an identified staff with a staff that has an unknown number of uses)

Mixer Monsters

  • Same as Synthesis Pot, but also lets you synthesize different category items together.
    (example: Dragon Grass followed by Plain Targe+1 → Plain Targe+1 with the Anti-Fire rune)
  • Mixers won't swallow thrown items while afflicted by certain status conditions.
    　Won't swallow: Napping, Asleep, Sound Asleep, Sealed, Paralyzed, S-Paralyzed, Onigiri, Tottering.
  • Mixers will turn toward the direction the thrown item came from when it swallows an item.
  • Any swallowed items will be placed on the ground when time of day switches and the Mixer vanishes.
  • See Runes for a list of item combinations to synthesize runes.

Retaining Item Blessing, Curse, Seal, and Identification

  • Generally, the state of the synthesized item takes on the state of the item that was inserted or thrown last.
    However, Mixer synthesis has priority based on item category, so you might end up with an undesired result.
    (If a weapon ends up cursed or sealed, synthesize the weapon with an Iron Arrow to remove it)

Mixer Synthesis Priority

Note: Item category priority is still being researched.

It seems Mixers synthesize items in a set order when multiple different category items are thrown after a weapon.
Weapons > Scrolls > Grass > Staves > Bracelets
This is the case no matter what order you throw items, so you may end up with unexpected results.
(Arrows, Talismans, and Pots haven't been checked yet)

For example, if you throw a weapon → staff (sealed) → grass, the resulting item will still be sealed
because grass items have a higher synthesis priority than staves.
So in this case, you'd need to synthesize twice to remove the seal.
(weapon + staff, then weapon + grass)

Common Mixer Synthesis Mistakes

  • Throwing your main weapon or shield at a napping Mixer.
    (Mixer mechanics changed from Shiren 4 onward, so always wake it up first)
  • The synthesized item was blown up by a Pop Tank monster after defeating the Mixer.
    (Always understand the risk of synthesizing on floors where Pop Tank monsters appear)
  • Synthesizing staves resulted in a sealed staff.
    (Always swing the staff you intend to synthesize last to verify that it's not sealed)

Tags

Shops in villages or dungeons can place a tag on equipment for a fee.
Tagged equipment will be delivered to the Sentry in Hermit's Hermitage if you lose the item.
※ You can only have up to 8 items tagged at the same time.
※ Mudkin monster special attacks can sometimes remove tags, so be careful.

Blessing / Curse / Seal

  • Blessed items are worth 10% more Gitan, and cursed or sealed items are worth 80% of their regular value.
  • Blessed bracelets and pots are protected from being cursed, sealed, or turned into onigiri.
    The blessing has a chance to wear off: "But X was fine because it was blessed." - Curse Trap, Curse Scroll, Nigiri Morph
    The blessing won't wear off: "...But the Curse was ineffective." - Curse Girls
  • Other item categories don't appear to have resistance against being sealed, even when blessed.
    (example: Blessed Revival Grass getting sealed by a Curse Girl)
  • The last item you throw at a Mixer when synthesizing determines the state of the resulting item.
    (example: Throw a cursed shield, then a normal shield, result will be normal)
  • Scoopies and Bored Kappas can't throw dirt or items into sealed pots.
    (Also, sealed Black Hole Pots will still create Pit Traps when shattered)

Resonance

Equipment can grant additional effects when certain items are equipped at the same time.
(example: equipping Ordinary Stick and Plain Targe at the same time lets you equip up to 2 bracelets)

See Resonance for a list of equipment combinations and effects.
