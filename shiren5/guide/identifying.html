Methods

Identification of items can be generally categorized into 5 main methods.

YOLO (You Only Live Once)

Technique where you use the item and determine what it is based on its effect.
It carries the risk of using a negative item, but is one of the basic methods of identification.

Every item category except bracelets and pots can be identified, but scrolls and grass items are consumed,
and you won't know how many uses a staff has left, so it's not a method that always guarantees complete identification.

It's best to perform this method right before you advance to the next floor to reduce the impact of negative effects.

Price

Technique where you narrow down possibilities based on an item's price.
It doesn't consume the item and there's zero risk, but doesn't guarantee identification.

You can gain insight into every item in your inventory, so players who learn how to do this will have a great advantage,
especially in dungeons like Primordial Chasm and Heavenly Lake where shops always appear on specific floors.

Narrowing down possibilities by price also makes it easier to apply other identification methods to the item.

Identify Scroll

Reliable method that guarantees complete identification of any item category.
It's important to make smart use of Identify Scrolls since you generally won't have too many opportunities to find them.

The ideal way to use an Identify Scroll is to give it to a Shopkeep to identify all merchandise inside a shop,
especially in dungeons where shops always appear on specific floors. (Note that the Identify Scroll must be identified)
If you have enough Gitan to buy items back, go ahead and sell your items before giving the scroll to identify more items.

If you choose to read the scroll, it's best to use it on item categories that are hard to identify using the YOLO method.
Many pots can be identified by inserting an item, and combined with price identification, you can identify nearly every pot.
So the cases in which you read the scroll on a pot are limited:

  • Synthesis Pot hasn't been identified yet, and you found a pot with a capacity of 5 and it'll be a while before you see a shop.
  • You found a pot with a capacity of 2 that could be a Blessing Pot or Dodger Pot, and want to avoid wasting a use.

On the other hand, there are many bracelets that can't be immediately identified when equipped, and there's
significant risk in equipping a cursed bracelet, so they're generally the best category for reading an Identify Scroll.

Reading an Identify Scroll occasionally results in a lucky use that identifies all items that aren't inside pots,
so it's best to take items out of pots and read it only when you have many unidentified items.

Identify Pot

Multiple items can be identified, but you need to shatter the pot to retrieve items, and it can't identify pots.
It's best to insert bracelets, but it's sometimes better to just use it instead of carrying it in anticipation of finding more bracelets
since it takes up an inventory slot that could be held by a different item.

If you've already identified key bracelets like Monsterphobic and Mojo Bracelet, go ahead and insert scrolls and grass.
It can also be used like an Ordinary Pot to hold items you won't need until later, such as items for Mixer synthesis.

Mixer Synthesis

Items that are successfully synthesized using Mixers are automatically identified in this game.
This is fantastic for grass items, since many grass items offer more value when synthesized compared to being used.

If one item used in synthesis is already identified, the resulting item will be identified as well.
This is especially important to remember when synthesizing staves to reveal use counts.
