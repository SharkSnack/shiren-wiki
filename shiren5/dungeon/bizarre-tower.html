Shiren 5 / Bizarre Tower

Bizarre Tower

Dungeon where you progress through 15 floor ranges using special rules in the order of your choice.
It's balanced so that the quality of items generally increases the further you progress.

The dungeon includes both day and night, but time of day doesn't change to night based on elapsed turns.
Field of view isn't restricted during the day with the exception of Monster House floor ranges,
and Monster Houses are never generated naturally outside of Monster House floor ranges.

There are 15 stairs representing floor ranges at the start, and you're free to challenge them in any order.
Any given floor range can only be chosen once, and you must go through all 15 floor ranges to clear the dungeon.

Strategy

General

Mixers appear between 8-10F, so plan a route that lets you collect items for synthesis prior to reaching that floor range.

Synthesis Pots are somewhat common, so if you're not struggling with synthesis, it's best to save the Mixer stairs
for the second half to get past floor ranges where trickier monsters normally spawn. (example: Ornery Tanks between 33-35F)
This greatly lowers the chance of encountering troublesome monsters, since the majority of spawns turn into Mixer monsters.

The Mixer floor range has a unique monster table. (30F+ is Mixermon, Mixergon, Kappa Pest, Green Zalokleft, and Concusschin)

Food, staves, and talismans can only be found on the ground within stairs 9-15 (See table in the first section),
so don't forget to check your remaining staff uses and food prior to choosing a new floor range.
Also, Gitan can only be found on the ground within stairs 8-12, so it's nice to visit 8-11 before 12.
However, stairs 9-10 are dangerous zones, so don't overdo it for the sake of Gitan.

Special Monster Houses are generated pretty often in Bizarre Tower,
so consider ignoring Monster Houses if you didn't start in one / if the stairs isn't located in one.
Doing so lowers risk, and lets you check other rooms without encountering many enemies.

It's good to visit stairs 1-2 after you've obtained Synthesis Pots or when it overlaps with natural Mixer spawns,
since you'll be able to synthesize the weapons and shields you find right away.

Identifying

See Identifying for identification tips.

Early Game

It's best to tackle Monster House floors early on, since HP regenerates quickly and dangerous monsters don't appear at first.
You'll also be able to collect a variety of items in time for the first Mixer zone (8-10F), helping with consistency.
However, Special Monster Houses are common, so expect to restart a number of times before things go smoothly.
For that reason, some players like to wait until they're able to defeat Mixers before attempting Monster House floors.

Maps between 6-10F don't have walls if they're Monster House floors, making it hard to collect arrows using Karakuroids (6-8F).
Therefore, some players like to postpone Monster House floors until after 8F so that they can collect more arrows.

Choose floors where HP doesn't regenerate around the time your HP regeneration slows down.
Check for traps and take things one step at a time, ideally keeping Super status active throughout the floors.
It's best to go after visiting stairs 3 (only grass) so you can stock up on Perception Grass and HP restoring grasses beforehand.

That said, it's not necessarily a bad idea to tackle stairs 9 and 10 right away.
You should be able to defeat most enemies in 1 or 2 hits in stairs 9, and you'll hardly take damage if you find a shield.
Monsters hit somewhat hard starting from 3F, so aim for a shield with at least 10 defense if possible.

Wood Arrow Traps appear between 1-5F, so walk along walls and check for traps to collect arrows.
The number of arrows you collect and strength of equipment comes down to luck when attempting stairs 10 early,
so if you don't find what you need pretty quickly, you'll likely end up collapsed.

εFO-UZZ can spawn on all floors during the night, and always drops blessed Revival Grass when defeated,
so another option is to proceed through stairs 11 early on in hopes of obtaining Revival Grass.

Mid Game

Avoid 3 (grass), 6-7 (pots), and 14-15 (Monster Houses) between Digestiphant (29-30F) and Kappa Pest (29-31F) floors.
Kappa Pest makes it difficult to collect items, so choose stairs that don't include valuable item categories.
Note that stairs 8 (only Gitan) is safe on Kappa Pest floors, since Lv2 Bored Kappas don't throw Gitan.

Late Game

Stairs 13 (Mixer monsters) can spawn powerful Mixergons starting on 30F, so it's usually not worth the risk to synthesize.
On the other hand, if you're prepared to rush stairs, choose stairs 13 when it overlaps with floors with dangerous enemies.
Examples: Ornery Tank (33-35F), MC Wizard (37-38F).

Rules

Only weapons and shields on the ground. (1 floor, 3 floors)

  • Popular choice to start with since you're guaranteed to find equipment.
  • Status inflicting weapons can't be found on the ground, so upgrade value will be the focus if chosen before Mixers (8-10F).

Only grass on the ground. (3 floors)

  • Easy rule since grasses are identified in this dungeon, but don't choose it when Grass Boy monsters appear.
  • Save Perception Grass you find for "HP doesn't regenerate, and lots of traps." floors.
  • Not a bad choice to overlap it with the first Mixer zone (8-10F).

Only scrolls on the ground. (1 floor, 3 floors)

  • Scrolls are unidentified, and bad scrolls like Swift Foe and Pot Dog can be found, so read scrolls while standing on the exit.
  • It's somewhat uncommon to find 2 copies of a scroll due to the large variety of scrolls that can be found,
    so it's best to prepare a way to identify them without using them.

Only pots on the ground. (1 floor, 3 floors)

  • Preservation Pots to expand inventory, Identify Pots to help identify other items, Fever Pots to duplicate items, etc.
  • Some like to start with Preservation Pots rather than wait until their inventory is full, but it comes down to preference.
  • Zalokleft Pots can also be found, so one option is to visit this zone before choosing the "Stores appear." floors.

Only Gitan on the ground. (4 floors)

  • Generally chosen before visiting the "Stores appear." floors.
  • Gitan bags you find typically range between 400-500G, so you can't use them to one-shot late game monsters.

HP doesn't regenerate, and lots of traps. (2 floors, 4 floors)

  • Perception Grass and Trap Deletion Scrl make the "lots of traps" portion a non-issue.
  • It's recommended to tackle these floors mid to late game, since HP regeneration slows down with higher max HP.
  • However, as mentioned earlier, some players prefer to clear them early while enemies aren't very threatening.
  • This floor range is also one of the few locations where Trap Del. Staff has a chance to shine.
  • So all said, it's up to you whether you want to tackle these early, or wait until the later when you can rush stairs.

It's always night. (3 floors)

  • Generally a bonus zone if you have a good set of abilities, but there are some dangerous floors such as 41-43F.
  • Some like to get these floors over with in the first half so that they don't need to carry torches.

Stores appear. (2 floors)

  • Nice option for the first Mixer zone (8-10F), since price identification lets you figure out possible synthesis ingredients.
  • Also a solid choice when you've collected lots of unidentified items and lack items to identify them in general.
  • Don't choose this floor range if dangerous monsters appear, since you might not have the leeway to shop.
  • If you're unlucky, the generated shops could be elite shops, meaning you won't be able to enter.

Mostly Mixer family monsters spawn. (5 floors)

  • Can be used to avoid floor ranges with troublesome monsters by turning most enemies into Mixer family monsters.
  • Be warned that higher level Mixers have high attack, so don't get careless trying to synthesize.

Monster Houses appear. (3 floors, 7 floors)

  • Encountering a Special Monster House near the start often ends the run, but waiting until the end can also be risky.
  • One counterplay would be to equip a torch and check what monsters are present before entering the room.
  • Some players go with 7 floors of Monster Houses right away, and then synthesize using Mixers afterward.
  • Others who like a thrill can choose these stairs last to enjoy 10 floors of late game Monster Houses.
  • In any case, it's best to avoid Tiger Tosser and Pumphantasm floors if challenging these stairs near the beginning.

Equipment

Weapon

Status inflicting weapons can only be found in shops, so they aren't likely to be a main weapon option.
Weapons that activate 2 bracelet resonance are solid choices, since many effective bracelets can be found on the ground.
Bladite and Red Blade are shop-exclusive, so you'll typically end up with a Dotanuki, Beast Fang, or Katana set.

Water Cutter or Scythe makes for an effective main weapon as well, but the other type effective weapons are a bit lackluster.

Weapon Runes

Prioritize status inflicting runes. (Paralyzing, Sedating, Boring, Confusing, etc.)
Anti-Aquatic and Anti-Floating are particularly effective among type effective runes, and Anti-Metal is nice for Ornery Tank.
Have a Karakuroid (6-8F) create an Iron Arrow Trap to obtain an Iron Arrow for synthesis purposes.

Shield

Targite and Red Shield are shop-exclusive, so you'll often end up with a Wolfshead, Beast Shield, or Iron Targe set.
Binary Shield makes for an effective main shield as well, provided you don't care to activate 2 bracelet resonance.
If you visit stairs 12 (Stores appear) near the beginning and find a Day Shield, don't hesitate to use it.

Shield Runes

Anti-Blast, Bit, Agile, and Anti-Theft are solid choices.
This dungeon only has a depth of 45 floors, so others like Anti-Fire, Anti-Peck, and Rustproof
aren't essential because level 3~4 Dragons, Gyadons, Mudkins, etc. don't make an appearance.

Bracelets

Monster Detector and Item Detector can be found on the ground, so pick up every unidentified bracelet.
If you start with Monster House floors, use traps such as Strip, Spin, and Sleep to safely equip and test for effects.
You can also visit stairs 12 (Stores appear) after you've collected a decent number of bracelets and try to identify by price,
or if you understand the risks of equipping a cursed bracelet, go ahead and equip them on the spot.

Other Items

Projectiles

Collect arrows using wood, iron, and poison arrow traps found in Monster Houses or created by Karakuroids (6-8F).
Poison Arrows make late game encounters easier, and you'll want at least 1 Iron Arrow for synthesis purposes.

Scrolls

Your chances to identify by price are limited, so you'll likely have to read the scroll or use an Identify Scroll or Identify Pot.
Scrolls with negative effects are common, so it's recommended to read scrolls while standing on the exit.
Simply reading a Pot Dog Scroll near the beginning of the adventure can end the run.

Staves

Swap and Pinning staves are effective on floors that don't have walls.
It's nice to have them for Monster House floor ranges in particular, since map generation changes in some cases.

Pots

Preservation, Heal, and fragrance pots are the essentials.
Fever Pots can be found on the ground, making them easier to obtain.

Talismans

Throwing a talisman carries some risk, but they're nice to have since scrolls are harder to identify in this dungeon.
Sleep and Fear talismans are recommended, since they effectively shut down most monsters.

Grass

It's good to carry some HP restoring grasses until you obtain items like Revival Grass, Swift Grass, and Heal Pot.
There's little need to carry other grasses, but Perception Grass is nice for floor ranges where HP doesn't regenerate.

Floor Guide

6-8F

Karakuroids appear, so collect arrows.
Remember, you need 1 Iron Arrow to add the Anti-Metal rune to your weapon.

6-10F

Maps in this floor range won't have walls if you choose stairs 14 or 15 (Monster Houses appear).
It's easy to collect items for synthesis going with Monster House floors, but it's trickier to collect arrows using traps.

Field of view is limited on Monster House floors, meaning you risk getting items cursed by Curse Girls in hallways,
and Fearabbits inside Monster Houses can end a run if you don't clear out enemies using arrows beforehand.
So all said, understand the added risk of choosing stairs 14 and 15 early on.

24-27F

Powerful enemies like Ironhead, Hipadile, Punisher, etc. begin to appear, so don't hesitate to use Poison Arrows.

33-35F

Ornery Tanks make an appearance, so rush stairs unless you have a way to deal with them.
One strategy is to choose stairs 11 (It's always night) to skip past them, but be warned that Evil Poofy appears on 33F.
Another option is the choose stairs 13 (Mostly Mixer family monsters), but Mixergon can be quite a threat too.

36F

This floor is low risk you have Rocks to deal with Cave Mamels, so hunt Grass Guys to stock up on grass.
That said, VeniScorp also appears, so be careful not to get stung if you don't have an Antidote Grass or Peach.
Reading an Immunity Scroll can also reduce the risk of accidents if you plan on lingering a long time.

37-38F

MC Wizards make an appearance, so rush stairs unless you have a way to deal with them.
Similar to Ornery Tanks, one strategy is to save stairs 11 (It's always night) until this floor range to skip past them,
but Bad Colocolums defeated by other night monsters can one-shot you by rolling into you if you're unlucky.

40-45F

This floor range is particularly dangerous at night.
41-43F includes Dark Scarabbit, Vile Pumpanshee, and Sinister Porkon as major threats.
44-45F is a bit better, but Dark Ornery Tanks appear so don't let your guard down too much.

For daytime, monsters to watch out for include Nashagga (40-42F), Sky Dragons (41-43F), and Nigiri Boss (44-45F).

Some players prefer to save stairs 13 (Mostly Mixer family monsters) until this final stretch to have an easier time.
