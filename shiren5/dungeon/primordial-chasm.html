Shiren 5 / Primordial Chasm

Primordial Chasm

Shiren 5's version of the series favorite unidentified items post-game dungeon.
It's always daytime, the item table features a wide variety of items, and wandering NPCs don't appear.
The first clear only requires you to reach 50F, and then the full 99F version is unlocked.
A shop always appears on 10F, 25F, 50F, and 75F, providing the player with additional strategies.

It's a standard dungeon, but the variety of strategies that can be used to clear it makes it extremely popular.
There are many weapon and shield options, and lots of ways to tailor the experience to your playstyle.
It tests techniques such as identification, synthesis, Nigiri Morph Factory, collecting arrows, rushing stairs, etc.
Grinding can be effective, but Wind of Kron and ominous auras both occur earlier than in other dungeons.

Strategy

Identifying

See Identifying for the basics.

It might be tempting to use items right away in order to identify them, but that's generally a bad idea.
Instead, you should save unidentified items until you come across a shop and identify by price,
since shops are guaranteed to appear on 10F, 25F, 50F, and 75F.

Imitation items such as Dracon Grass only appear starting on 18F in Primordial Chasm,
so some players simply ignore unidentified items after 17F if they managed to identify the essentials.

Grass

Eat grass only if you find a duplicate copy, and identify the others by synthesizing and checking prices on 10F.
Grasses used for synthesis are in the price range of 100G, 300G, and 500G. (Check 2000G for Invincible Grass)

Items are automatically identified if they're successfully synthesized, and Revival Grass is somewhat common,
so eating grasses to identify them usually results in missed opportunities.
If a grass failed synthesis, go ahead and eat the grass to identify it - but beware that it could be Rage Grass.
Don't eat 500G grasses that failed synthesis on floors where a shop is present.

50G = Herb or Fine Grass, 200G = Heal Grass, 1000G = Revival or Gut grass, 1500G = Undo or Repeat grass.
Since imitation items only appear starting on 18F, 1000G and 1500G grasses are always Revival and Undo grass
if they're found between 1-17F.

Amnesia Grass and Imabikiso can appear in shops starting on 12F, and SuperUnlucky Seed starting on 30F.
Snacky, Munchy, and Maneater drop shop table items, so don't let your guard down.

Bracelets

It's fine to equip unidentified bracelets between 1-8F since Karakuroids (6-8F) can create Strip Traps,
but wait until you encounter a shop to identify by price from 9F onward.

Don't equip unidentified bracelets starting on 18F, since they could be Itemphobic, Dozer Bracelet, etc.

Important Items

Weapon, Shield

See Rune Details to get an idea of synthesis priority.

Monster Detector, Wall Clip Bracelet, and Heal Bracelet are the 3 "sacred treasures" of the dungeon,
but Wall Clip Bracelet is exclusively found in elite shops, so you're unlikely to obtain it.
Therefore, equipment sets that activate 2 bracelet resonance aren't a high priority.

Another reason is that Day Shield can be found despite it being a daytime-only dungeon,
meaning you can main the strongest shield in the game without any drawbacks.

The ideal pairing is any weapon that gains âˆž rune slots at Lv8 + Day Shield.
Dirk of Debts is also extremely powerful, especially if you're unable to find a Day Shield.
Binary Shield can be used as an alternative, but it's a bit tricky to manage your HP to make the most of it.
Shields other than Day Shield and Pauper's Plank basically aren't effective at reducing damage late game.

Also, it's good to remember that certain shields gain useful runes as they level up.
Plain Targe = CR Diet (Lv8), Binary Shield = Magi-Twister (Lv6), Red Shield = Anti-Peck (Lv8), etc.
It's easy to level equipment during the second half using the Tinkerer effect from Super status.

Dotanuki, Bladite, and Red Blade all have good base attack and rune counts while leveling up.
If you consistently steal from shops, Dirk of Debts is a strong option that one-shots most enemies even late game.
That said, it's often better as a secondary weapon between 30-74F since you risk running out of Gitan.
Between the guaranteed shop on 75F, Gitan Mamels and Kleptoads on 80-82F, and Ultra Gazers on 88-93F,
you shouldn't have any issues wielding it near the end even at Lv8 (250G per hit).

Bracelets

  • Monster Detector, Monsterphobic
    • Field of view is normally limited in Primordial Chasm, so this drastically lowers the difficulty of the dungeon.
      Generally increases in value the deeper you progress, since late game enemies hit harder.
      Monsterphobic is still usable, so don't discard it thinking it's a bad item.
  • Item Detector, Itemphobic
    • Lets you plan an efficient route to check rooms, and informs you of shops and Monster Houses at a glance.
      It's not as high priority as Monster Detector or Heal Bracelet, but it has its uses.
  • Cleansing Bracelet
    • Protects against Mutaikon and Scorpion special attacks between 11-12F.
      It's rare to find a Cleansing Bracelet early into the run, but it's incredibly useful for these 2 floors.
      It's not essential once you're past 12F, but can be helpful against higher level Mutaikons if you
      weren't able to find the corresponding bracelet before reaching their floor range.
  • Anti-Cnf. Bracelet
    • Protects against Spin Traps and Blade Bee and Dazikon special attacks,
      along with MC Mage and MC Wizard's magic bullets that inflict Confused status.
      MC Wizard is particularly dangerous since they can inflict Berserk status at the same time.
      Confused status usually isn't too dire of a status condition, but preventing it lets you conserve items,
      so it's good to keep it on hand until you make it past Dazikon (34-35F) and MC Wizard (37-38F) floors.
      Some players like to use it until the end when they're using a Baffle Axe as a main weapon.
  • Alert Bracelet
    • Protects against Naptapir yawns, Dozikon (67-68F) special attacks, and Sleep Traps.
      Likely the best option to keep equipped among status condition preventing bracelets.
  • Anti-Crs. Bracelet
    • Shop-exclusive. Turns Curse Girls into generic enemies.
      Keep it equipped on Horrabbit (56-58F) floors to avoid surprise curses by Curspinster.
