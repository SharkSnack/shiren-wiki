Shiren 5 / Onigiri Hollow

Onigiri Hollow

Dungeon where the map layout is always visible, and rooms collapse when you exit them.

Shops and the room with the exit stairs will never collapse.
Traps and water tiles inside rooms vanish when the room collapses, but items and monster drops remain.

Onigiri can't be found, but can be obtained from Nigiri Baby and Kumonigiri families, and Decay Traps.
Diet Shield can't be found, but Plain Targe appears so it's still possible to obtain the CR Diet rune.

Only equipment with innate abilities can be found on the ground.
Equipment that activates 2 bracelet resonance and type effective weapons are mostly shop-exclusive.
Rusty Pickaxe, Boring Staff, Warp Grass, Black Hole Pot, and Fort. Staff are pretty common.

Wonder Pick can only be found in shops between 90-99F of this dungeon in the base game.
(It can be obtained by simply clearing Merchant's Hideout on PS Vita and Switch / Steam)

Strategy

The dungeon's characteristic limits the routes you can take to check rooms, so look at the map first and think carefully.
If the route is a one-way path, you won't be able to retreat into the hallway you came from once you enter a room,
so unless you have an item like Boring Staff to dig yourself out, expect to fight all enemies in the room at once.

Collapsing Rooms

All rooms besides shops and the stairs room collapse when you exit them, no matter how you left the room.
Traps and monsters in the room vanish when the room collapses, but items will remain buried in the walls,
so it's possible to dig the items out using items like pickaxes or Boring Staff after the room collapses.
This can be used to obtain island items, and items dropped by enemies such as FO-UZZ and Mixers.

Avoiding Game Overs

It's possible to find yourself in a situation where there's no way to advance to the next floor in this dungeon.

Many things can hinder a route, and the following can cause a game over if you don't have items to recover:

  • Warping to an isolated room due to a Spring Trap, DJ Mage, Grass Boy, or higher level Bored Kappa.
  • Moving to a dead end hallway due to a Fearabbit, Yanpii, Tiger Tosser, Log Trap, or Berserk status.

Pickaxes, Boring Staff, Warp Grass, and Black Hole Pot are the basics to recover from these situations.
All four items are commonly found in Onigiri Hollow and are easy to use without major drawbacks.
These can also be used to help you navigate your route or to escape bad situations in general.

  • Boring Staff: Creates a 10 tile deep tunnel when the magic bullet hits a wall. Found with 4~6 uses.
  • Pickaxe: Can be used to dig through walls, but has a chance to break while digging.
  • Warp Grass: Warps you to a different room on the current floor. Beware of this grass if eating unidentified grasses.
  • Black Hole Pot: A number of Pit Traps equal to capacity appear when it shatters. Found with 2~4 capacity.

Other ways to recover from a game over situation:

  • Land on a water or air tile using a Pinning Staff, Swap Staff, or Staff of Sacrifice to warp.
  • Charge through wall tiles using a Wall Clip Bracelet and HP restoring items as needed.
  • Step onto a water tile using a Waterwalk Bracelet, and then unequip the bracelet to warp.
  • Get hit by Yanpii's headbutt to land on a water or air tile to warp.
  • Get hit by the magic bullet from a DJ Mage or MC Mage to warp.
  • Destroy wall tiles using an Explosion Bracelet or Pop Tank family monster.
  • Equip a Blink Bracelet to warp.

Blink Bracelet in particular can be used an unlimited number of times, and lets you check all rooms on each floor.
However, you'll still want to carry Black Hole Pots or items that can dig walls in case the bracelet gets sealed.

※ Sticky Pot has the potential to cause a game over if your hand gets stuck while inside a closed off room.

Food Management

Food management is a secondary theme of Onigiri Hollow that helps give the dungeon its unique flavor.

Onigiri generally can't be found, so peaches will be your main food source.
Preservation Pots are uncommon, making it difficult to manage peach ripeness.
At the same time, there are a number of floors where you'll want to linger and farm if possible,
so it's tricky to find the right balance between farming and avoiding starvation early to mid game.

The following 3 items are key to alleviating hunger issues:

  • Preservation Pot: Store peaches inside to keep them from ripening into Rotten Peaches.
  • Inacc. Bracelet: Create Rotten Onigiri using Decay Traps.
    Make use of Karakuroid (6-10F), Steamroid (22-24F), Electroid (49-53F), and Cyberoid (72-77F).
  • Dodger Pot: Use it to create Rotten Onigiri like Inacc. Bracelet, and to collect arrows.

The following 5 items are also helpful:

  • Plain Targe: It gains the CR Diet rune at Lv8. Obtained from Presto Pots or Zaloklefts. (Diet Shield doesn't appear)
  • Water Pot: Can be used to ripen any peach into a Juicy Peach, except for Rotten Peach. Shop-exclusive.
  • Waterwalk Bracelet: Same as Water Pot. Shop-exclusive.
  • Unlucky Staff: Level down a Nigiri Boss (44-45F) or Nigiri King (86-91F) to perform Nigiri Morph Factory.
  • Level up items: Level up a Nigiri Baby (6-8F) to perform Nigiri Morph Factory. (Higher priority than Gitan Mamel)

Nigiri Morph Factory is the most consistent way to obtain food, so it's best to plan ahead and perform it on 22F.
Decay Traps are another reliable food source, so keep an eye out for them when collecting arrows using Karakuroids.
Inacc. Bracelet or Dodger Pot can be used alongside Strip and Hunger traps to weaken Karakuroids and Nigiri Morphs.

Be careful how you handle the Nigiri Morph when you encounter one - use a Paralysis or Transient staff
if you need more time to prepare, as opposed to defeating it and hoping for another spawn once you're ready.
If you're unlucky with monster spawns, you could end up starving even if you started with around 50 fullness.

Nigiri Babies appear between 6-8F but have a low spawn rate on 8F due to Mixers and others being added to the table.
Similarly, Nigiri Morphs have a noticeably lower spawn rate on 23F compared to 22F, so treat 8F and 23F as backups
in case you step on a Pit Trap, and aim to perform the technique on earlier floors.

If it's not possible to create a route that leaves only the stairs room on floors you want to linger and farm on,
consider farming in the starting room before checking any others rooms instead.
However, napping enemies can block room exits, so it's also important to call it quits if you're not seeing results.

Monster Generation

Generally, 1 monster is generated every 30 turns inside a room where Shiren isn't present.
If the stairs room is the only room left on the floor, monsters can still spawn outside of Shiren's view.
Monster density can be somewhat controlled by collapsing rooms, but if you take too long,
the stairs room can end up being packed with monsters by the time you arrive.
Lastly, collapsing all rooms lets you efficiently farm monsters like Maneaters.

Controlling Stairs Room Spawns

Monsters stop spawning if the stairs room is the only room left and Shiren is currently in the room.
However, there's an exception to this where monsters will continue to spawn even if Shiren is in the room,
which occurs when you destroy a wall tile after the stairs room becomes the only room left on the current floor.

This can lead to some problematic situations:

  • You'd like to step in place to heal HP before advancing to the next floor, but monsters keep spawning.
  • Enemy spawns get in the way of safely performing techniques like Nigiri Morph Factory.

Conversely, there are ways to take advantage of it:

  • Zaloklefts and other napping types will be generated with Napping status even if you're in the room.
  • You can stand on a Sanctuary Scroll away from a room entrance to safely spawn monsters.

So, if you want to dig paths but don't want monsters to spawn in the stairs room,
simply destroy the wall tiles before the stairs room becomes the last room on the current floor.

If the room is small enough to be entirely visible on screen, monsters won't spawn even with the above condition.
Position Shiren in the center of the room to prevent spawns, and move him to a corner or hallway to allow spawns.

Monster Houses

Pop-up Monster Houses don't appear in Onigiri Hollow, but Special Monster Houses can be generated.

Stairs Room

Easy to collect items, since the room doesn't collapse when you retreat into a hallway.
Can be conquered in a similar fashion to Monster Houses in other dungeons,
but you might have fewer escape options thanks to dead end hallways.

Non-Stairs Room

The room collapses when you exit the room, so you can easily escape from otherwise dire Monster Houses.
However, you can't retreat if you want to collect items, so it can be quite tricky to overcome the situation.
It helps to thin out enemies before entering the room using arrows, rocks, staves, talismans, etc.
Pickaxes and Boring Staff can be used to dig in ways that lets you shoot projectiles from additional angles.
If an enemy is near the room's entrance, you can use a Hilarious Pot to wake up the entire room before entering.

Other Shopkeepers won't be able to reach you since the room collapses behind you,
so you're free to steal items and advance to the next floor using a Black Hole Pot when you're done.
It's also possible to dig a path to the stairs room once you're done stealing items, but it's a bit risky.

Character Limit

Method that takes advantage of the fact that only 20 characters can be present on a floor at a time.
Collapse rooms in a way that leaves one room that isn't connected to the shop or stairs room,
and then step in place in the stairs room to spawn monsters in the disconnected room.

When thief mode is triggered, the enemies in the disconnected room will turn into Shopkeepers,
and new Shopkeepers won't spawn elsewhere due to the character limit.
However, Shopkeepers transformed from enemies that were on a water, air, or wall tile will warp.

Keep in mind that this technique requires at least 60 fullness to perform.
If you're short on food, it's best to use a different method, purchase items normally, or skip the shop.

Equipment

Weapons found on the ground are typically Rusty Pickaxes or status inflicting weapons,
and 2 bracelet resonance and type effective weapons are exclusive to shops, Presto Pots, and Zaloklefts.

Weapon

  • Rusty Pickaxe
    • High rune count and levels up extremely quickly. Just be careful not to accidentally hit a wall.
  • Dirk of Debts
    • Incredibly powerful weapon, and Gitan isn't an issue since it's easy to steal from shops in Onigiri Hollow.

Weapon Runes

  • Tri-direction
    • Field of view isn't limited, so it's easy to take advantage of the ability to attack around corners.
      However, the inability to retreat can hinder its usage on occasion.
  • Anti-Floating
    • Warp Grass is common, so it's easy to synthesize this rune.
  • Anti-Aquatic
    • Scrolls are always found identified, so it's a bit easier to synthesize this rune compared to other dungeons.
  • Anti-Dragon
    • Dragon Grass is basically exclusive to shops, so it's difficult to synthesize this rune.

Shield

  • Day Shield
    • Strongest shield in the game for daytime dungeons. Use it as a main shield if you obtain it.
  • Binary Shield
    • Not as strong as Day Shield, but it's the next best option among shields that can be found on the ground.
      Gains the Magi-Twister rune at Lv6.
  • Red Shield
    • Only found in shops or elite shops.
      Gains the Anti-Fire rune at Lv8, making it worth leveling up even if you don't use it as a main shield.
  • Lock Shield, Safe Shield, Gyadon Blocker
    • Most shields found on the ground are very weak aside from Day Shield and Binary Shield,
      but sometimes you won't find a better option than one of these to use as a main shield.
      On the plus side, all three gain a defensive rune at Lv8.

Shield Runes

  • Anti-Hypno
    • Anti-Gaze Trge is exclusive to elite shops.
      Ultra Gazer appears for 11 floors in a row near the end, so synthesize this rune if at all possible.
  • Magi-Twister
    • Swap Shield is exclusive to elite shops, but this rune can also be obtained by leveling a Binary Shield to Lv6.
  • Anti-Peck
    • Helps against higher level Gyadon family monsters.
  • Anti-Fire
    • Difficult to synthesize compared to other dungeons because Snake Shield is shop-exclusive,
      and Dragon Grass can only be obtained from shops, Presto Pots, or Zaloklefts.
  • Anti-Blast
    • Blast Shield is shop-exclusive, but this rune can also be obtained by leveling a Lock Shield or Safe Shield to Lv8.
  • CR Diet
    • Since Diet Shield can't be found, the only way to obtain this rune is to level a Plain Targe to Lv8.
      Hunt enemies like Maneaters while you have Tinkerer status to quickly level the shield,
      and then synthesize it once you reach Mixermon floors (31-33F).
  • Anti-Blast※
    • Fort. Staff is somewhat common and scrolls are identified, so it's easier to synthesize than in other dungeons.
      Explosion Bracelet is useful for avoiding game over situations, so think carefully before synthesizing it.

Bracelets

  • Blink Bracelet
    • Extremely powerful since it speeds up checking rooms, and eliminates the risk of game over situations.
      The only weakness it has is becoming sealed, and that you can't control when it activates or where you land.
  • Wall Clip Bracelet
    • Exclusive to elite shops.
      Use it to move and attack through corners, and to escape otherwise game over situations.
      Can be used alongside a Fort. Staff or item that destroys walls to create a safe spot to attack enemies.
  • Item Detector, Itemphobic
    • Helps you plan a more informed room checking route at the start of a floor.
      Also lets you locate shops and items that were generated in walls.
  • Monster Detector, Monsterphobic
    • Field of view isn't limited in this dungeon, but these bracelets still help you navigate around enemies.
      Also lets you locate shops at a glance.
  • Explosion Bracelet
    • Lets you destroy wall tiles at the exchange of half of your HP per explosion.
      Can also be used to one-shot Shopkeepers, and grill onigiri without losing Super status.
  • Alert Bracelet
    • Nice to have on Dozikon floors (67-68F).
  • Monster Summoner
    • Improves farming efficiency when hunting monsters.
  • Trap Bracelet
    • It's easy to locate newly generated traps when the only room left on the floor is the stairs room.
  • Inacc. Bracelet
    • Lets you make use of traps created by Karakuroid family monsters (6-10F, 22-24F, 49-53F, 72-77F).
      Food can be an issue in Onigiri Hollow, so this bracelet is quite valuable.

Other Items

Projectiles

  • Rock, Porky Rock
    • Used to wake up monsters near the entrances of rooms.
  • Poison Arrow
    • Collect them between 6-10F using Karakuroids.
      A single arrow roughly halves the damage you receive from an enemy's regular attack.

Scrolls

  • Immunity Scroll
    • Makes you immune to Ultra Gazer's hypnosis, the paralyzing effect of Maneater roars, etc.
      It's good to keep 1 on hand at all times in case you find the other items needed to synthesize the Anti-Blast※ rune.
  • Vacuum Slash Scrl
    • Useful when you want to avoid retreating into a hallway.
  • Collection Scroll
    • Works on items buried in walls, so it's a quick way to collect Monster House items even if the room collapsed.
  • Desert Scroll
    • Used as a synthesis ingredient to create the Anti-Aquatic rune.
      Can occasionally be used to escape a game over situation if there are waterways on the floor.

Staves

  • Boring Staff
    • It's best to carry as many of these as you can, grouping them together using Mixers when possible.
      Used to avoid game over situations, dig escape routes, misdirect pursuing monsters, obtain buried items, etc.
  • Pinning Staff
    • Escape from a game over situation by landing on a water or air tile to warp.
  • Transient Staff
    • Send useful monsters like Nigiri Morph, Mixer, and Boy Cart to the stairs until you're ready to utilize them.
      Can be used alongside a Reflection Pot to escape from a game over situation.
  • Swap Staff
    • Switch places with a target in a hallway from a room to bury them alive. (example: elite shop Shopkeeper)
      Also used to warp by switching places with a monster on a water or air tile.
  • Knockback Staff
    • Use alongside a Reflection Pot to escape from a game over situation, if there are water or air tiles.
  • Staff of Sacrifice
    • Another option to escape from a game over situation, and useful when you need to run away from enemies.
  • Unlucky Staff
    • Lets you perform Nigiri Morph Factory on Nigiri Boss (44-45F) or Nigiri King (86-91F) floors,
      which is particularly nice if you're planning on hunting Isleaters for a Wonder Pick.

Pots

  • Preservation Pot
    • Useful for preventing peaches from turning into Rotten Peaches. Somewhat uncommon.
  • Black Hole Pot
    • Lets you escape from a game over situation, steal from a shop without risk, skip past dangerous floors, etc.
      Pit Traps appear even if the pot is sealed, so always seal it if you have the opportunity to protect the pot
      from Bored Kappas and Scoopies, and to avoid losing important items due to Gazer hypnosis.
      Sealing the pot also contributes toward the critical hit rate of Mojo Bracelet.
  • Reflection Pot
    • Counters DJ Mages and Gazers, and very nice to have for the Ultra Gazer floors near the end.
      Can be used alongside a variety of staves to escape from game over situations.
  • Dodger Pot
    • Counters Mutaikon and Porky monsters, and is also used to collect arrows from Boy Carts.
      Field of view isn't limited, so collecting Knockback Arrows makes your adventure significantly easier.
  • Zen Pot
    • Makes you immune to Dragon and Pop Tank special attacks.
      Also lets you destroy walls using an Explosion Bracelet without taking damage.
      However, Abyssal Dragons doesn't appear, and Pop Tank family monsters only appear on 5 floors.
  • Water Pot
    • Shop-exclusive. One splash of water shuts down dangerous monsters like Shaggas and Punishers.
      Also used to ripen a peach by 1 stage. Water tiles are somewhat uncommon, so it can be a struggle to refill it.

Floor Guide

Early Game (1-21F)

There are few options to avoid game over situations at the start, so you might not be able to check all rooms.

1-5F

Floors generate 7~8 items until 3F, so hurry to the next floor once you've collected them to conserve fullness.
If you find Gitan, step on it and swap it with an inventory item instead of picking it up.
Throwing Gitan lets you easily defeat Grass Boys or higher level Mamels.

1. Level up a Pit Mamel (3-5F)
Create a Cave Mamel or Gitan Mamel, and then use Rocks or Gitan to defeat them to gain lots of exp.
Thrown items can miss, so it's best to throw from a distance or use a staff.
If you're using a Glorious Staff, save one use for Nigiri Morph Factory on 6F.
Berserker and Glorious talismans can't be found, so there are few opportunities to use this technique.

2. Swing Staves at Pit Mamels from a Distance
The first reason is that if the staff was a Glorious Staff, one of its uses won't go to waste.
The second reason is that Unlucky Staff can be identified since Pit Mamel is a Lv2 monster.

3. Grass Boys (3-5F)
Grass Boys always drop grass items, helping you progress identification and obtain synthesis ingredients.
In particular, you'll want to identify Warp Grass as soon as possible.
Throw 280+ Gitan or use Rocks or arrows in hallways to defeat it without giving it a chance to act.
Go ahead and linger for a while if you have extra food, but beware of getting hit by Warp Grass.

4. Managing Peach Ripeness
If you don't have a Preservation Pot, you'll just have to eat peaches before they turn into Rotten Peaches.
It takes 3 floors to ripen a Hard Peach into a Peach, and 2 floors for the others.

6-10F

1. Karakuroids (6-10F)
Useful monster that creates visible traps, and appears in a wider range of floors compared to other dungeons.
It's rare to obtain a strong shield this early, so you'll need a way to reduce the amount of damage you take.

  • Eat a Hard Peach to increase defense.
  • Reduce its action speed with a Slow Staff or Slow Talisman.
  • Throw an item while it's on a Slow, Hunger, or Strip trap in hopes of it missing and triggering the trap.
  • Shoot Poison Arrows.

...and so on.

There's a 64 trap limit to the number of traps that can be present, at which point Karakuroids stop creating traps.
Trap Deletion Scrl and Trap Del. Staff can be used to erase unneeded traps so that new traps can be created.

Useful traps include the following:

  • Wood Arrow, Iron Arrow, Poison Arrow
    • Collecting arrows is the primary reason to utilize Karakuroids.
      Poison Arrows reduce the amount of damage enemies deal, and are used for Nigiri Morph Factory.
      Be sure to obtain at least 1 Iron Arrow to synthesize the Anti-Metal rune.
      Have the Karakuroid create traps along the wall if you don't have Rocks.
  • Strip
    • Equip weapons, shields, and bracelets to check upgrade values and effects without worrying about curses.
  • Decay
    • Trigger the trap while an enemy is standing on it to create Rotten Onigiri.
      Inacc. Bracelet, Dodger Pot, and Killer Arrow are the best, but you can simply throw anything and hope it misses.
      Throwing a pot with an item inside will result in the trap being triggered whether it misses or hits the target.
      Use an Explosion Trap, Explosion Bracelet, etc. to turn Rotten Onigiri into Grilled Onigiri.
  • Explosion
    • Step on it to transform Rotten Onigiri into Grilled Onigiri.
  • Spin, Sleep
    • Check if an unidentified bracelet is an Anti-Cnf. Bracelet or Alert Bracelet.
  • Curse
    • Seal items to increase the critical hit rate of Mojo Bracelet.
    • Seal a Shoddy Dirk or Shoddy Plank to use the equipment without lowering upgrade value.
    • Change an equipped item's curse to a seal so that you can unequip the item.
      Always place all other items on the ground before stepping on the trap.

2. Nigiri Morph Factory (6-8F)
Level up a Nigiri Baby using a Glorious Staff, Rage Grass, Clone Staff, etc.
For safety, it's best to perform it in the stairs room after collapsing all other rooms.
This lets you avoid dangerous monsters like Tiger Tossers, and some players prefer to do it on 8F
so that they can synthesize using Mixers to free up inventory space and carry more onigiri.
However, if the stairs room is large, monsters might still spawn even if it's the last room on the floor.

3. Maneaters (5-7F)
The first floor range where ominous auras can occur is 5-7F, which overlaps with Nigiri Baby floors.
There's little danger if the stairs room is the only room left and you're standing in the room,
and it seems the only floor where Maneaters have a higher chance of appearing is 7F.

4. Mixers (8-10F)
Synthesize to upgrade equipment, and don't forget to combine staves.
Mixers have the highest spawn rate on 9F, so it's recommended to synthesize on that floor.
Note that there isn't a guaranteed shop on 10F in Onigiri Hollow.

11-21F

Mutaikons appear between 11-12F.
Replenish fullness by having a Mutaikon throw Poison Grass at you if you have a Cleansing Bracelet.

12F has combination of troublesome monsters like Mudkin, Swordsman, Mutaikon, and Scorpion.
Field of view isn't limited so you shouldn't have too much trouble, but be careful not to lose your shield.

Snacky can appear between 13-15F, and drops a shop table item when defeated.
It's possible to obtain items like Breeze Blade or Heal Bracelet, so linger and hunt them if you have extra food.
However, be warned that Ominous Auras can occur between 14~16F.

Zaloklefts appear between 17-18F, so linger and hunt them if you have enough food to do so.
It's most convenient to collapse all rooms besides the stairs room when hunting them.
Scoopies also appear, so place pots on the ground in a hallway to protect them.

DJ Mages appear between 19-21F, and can potentially cause a game over situation by making you warp.
Try to avoid getting hit be their magic bullet as much as possible.

Grass Bros (20-22F), Green Zaloklefts (21-23F), and Nigiri Morphs (22-23F) appear.
Perform Nigiri Morph Factory and linger until the wind blows to obtain items.

Boy Carts appear between 20-21F as well, and can be leveled up into a Strong Cart for Knockback Arrows.
You'll have another chance later on, but a Scoopie family monster could ruin your Dodger Pot
before you reach that floor range, so it's best to collect arrows here.

Mid Game (22-51F)

As mentioned above, Nigiri Morphs appear between 22-23F. (See Nigiri Morph Factory for details)
Always collapse all other rooms besides the stairs room before performing the technique in Onigiri Hollow,
because otherwise a Grass Bro can cause an accident by throwing Warp Grass.
Note that ominous auras don't occur on Nigiri Morph floors in this dungeon.

Sr. Yanpii (26-28F) can potentially cause a game over if they headbutt you into a wall from inside a room,
so use the Scout command and avoid lining up with them if they're present.
Also, you won't get pushed into the wall if you get hit diagonally.
Spadies also appear, so it may be best to hurry to the stairs.

Villeaters appear between 25-27F, so if you plan to hunt them, do so on 26F to avoid Grampa Tanks and Explochins.
This is the ideal time to level up equipment such as Plain Targe or Binary Shield for their added runes.
Place pots on the ground if you plan on lingering for a while to protect them from Spadies.

Cross Carts appear between 28-29F, so you have another chance to collect arrows.
Again, it's best to collapse all other rooms besides the stairs room beforehand.

Mixermons (31-33F) appear alongside power monsters like Katana Bee, Shagga, Dragon, and Death Gyaza.
Sanctuary Scrolls can be used to synthesize items with minimal risk, and all of these monsters offer a lot
of equipment experience points, so if it's a great spot to level equipment if you have Super status.
MC Mages appear on 31F, so it's best to farm monsters between 32-33F instead.

Dazikons (34-35F) can be used to replenish fullness if you have an Anti-Cnf. Bracelet.

Ornery Tanks and Scarabbits appear between 35-36F, so aim to fight enemies in hallways instead of rooms.

Grass Guys spawn between 36-38F and can be used to perform Grass Factory.
That said, it's questionable if it's worth doing, since Strength Grass isn't very common in this dungeon.
If you decide to perform it, do so in the stairs room after collapsing all other rooms to prevent Warp Grass accidents.
If you have a Fort. Staff, you can trap a Grass Guy in the corner and simply equip a Cleansing Bracelet.

MC Wizard appears between 37-38F, and can cause a game over if their magic bullet inflicts Berserk status.

Munchy can spawn between 45-46F, but it's best to only hunt them on 46F to avoid Nigiri Boss (44-45F).

Porkon floors (49-50F) overlap with Trowelies and Strong Carts, so use a Dodger Pot if you have one.
However, Super Gazers also appear on 49F, so some players prefer not to use a Dodger Pot on that floor
so that they can use projectiles to deal with Super Gazers. In either case, it's best to hurry to the stairs on 49F.
If you intend to collect arrows, do so in the stairs room after collapsing all other rooms.

Mixergons appear between 50-51F, so finish synthesizing equipment and group staves together.
N'machs spawn on these floors too, so it's best to either ignore items, or insert them into designated pots
so that you can easily tell which items in your inventory carry the risk of being a N'mach.

Electroids appear between 49-53F, and can be used to obtain onigiri or trap other monsters using a Dodger Pot.
In particular, some players like to use traps to hunt Mounteaters (51-53F).
