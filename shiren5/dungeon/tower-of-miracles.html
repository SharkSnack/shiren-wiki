Shiren 5 / Tower of Miracles

Tower of Miracles

Final dungeon of the main story, continued from Tower of Fortune.

Powerful monsters appear in an effort to live up to being the finale of the story.
Similar to Tower of Fortune, this dungeon is split into areas with a skywalk zone in the middle.
Tower areas are Random Dungeon type maps, and the skywalk is Shuffle Dungeon type maps.

An overworld resting area called Sparrow's Inn is located after 3F.
It includes an inn, item shop, and warehouse, so be sure to take a moment to prepare for the final stretch.
Staying at the inn occasionally increases max HP, max strength, or max fullness.

Strategy

Equipment

Important Runes

Anti-Dragon
Effective against Nashaggas and Sky Dragons.
However, it's dangerous to trade attacks with them even if you have this rune.
Synthesize it by throwing a weapon and Dragon Grass at a Mixer.
Sedating, Paralyzing, Confusing
You want to avoid getting hit as much as possible, so these runes help prevent the target from attacking back.
Synthesize them by throwing a weapon and Sleepy Grass, Paralysis Staff, or Confusion Grass at a Mixer.
Anti-Fire
Mainly used to halve fire damage from Sky Dragons, but also helps against Flamepuffs.
Synthesize it by throwing a shield and Dragon Grass at a Mixer.

Floor Guide

Tower Area 1 (1-3F)

There are fewer starting enemies, and lots of items on the ground.
Hidden hallways are common, and can leave unfamiliar players feeling like they encountered an unwinnable map.
Dash along the wall, and if Shiren stops before reaching the other end, there's a hidden hallway.
Once you've identified the entrance, simply perform a direct attack at the wall to reveal the hallway.

Cave Mamels appear between 2-3F, and only take 1 damage from direct attacks.
You can deal damage normally if you inflict Sealed status, but it's best to save Seal Staff and Seal Talismans for later.
So instead, use items that deal fixed damage like Rock, Porky Rock, and Electric Staff to one-shot them.
They have 6 HP, so a Knockback Staff use (5 damage if it hits a wall) + one direct attack can defeat them as well.
Another option is to equip a Counter Shield (Retribution rune), but be warned that Cave Mamels hit hard.

Dazikons appear between 2-3F, and can be tricky to deal with.
Use ranged attacks like arrows to defeat them before they get close enough to throw Confusion Grass at you. (2 tile radius)
Approaching with direct attacks will likely cause you to lose Super status and strength.
If you have an Anti-Cnf. Bracelet or Alert Bracelet, try to obtain Confusion Grass or Sleepy Grass for synthesis.
Dark Mixermons appear between 5-6F, so you still have one more chance to synthesize items before the boss.
You can level up a Dazikon into a Dozikon by using items like Clone Staff, Cheery Grass, Fear Scroll, etc.

Evil Sky Dragons appear between 1-3F at night, and can deal 50 fire damage from anywhere in the room.
It's best to avoid them by hurrying through these 3 floors before it changes to night, even at the cost of skipping some items.
If you don't make it through before it changes to night, it's recommended to wait for daytime in a hallway.

Tower Skywalk (4-6F)

Shuffle Dungeon type map where you step on floor pads that send you flying to different sections of the map.
It's easy to get separated from your allies, so do your best to keep an eye on them so that they don't get defeated.
Hidden traps can be generated on destination tiles as well, so it's a bit scary.

MC Wizards appear between 4-5F, and are extremely dangerous.
They fire magic bullets much more frequently than their night counterparts, and can inflict Berserk status.
Shiren is very likely to defeat allies if he goes Berserk, and might be defeated by an ally if his shield is weak.
The Cloned magic bullet effect will inflict Shiren with Confused status, limiting his movement options.
Use Reflection Pot or Immunity Scroll - this is the last area with Magic type monsters, so go ahead and use them up.

Dark Mixermons appear between 5-6F at night.
This is your last chance to synthesize items during the main story without a Synthesis Pot.
Dark Ironhead hits hard, so you'll want a Monster Detector, Super Torch, or Navigation Scroll.
It's best to synthesize on 6F to avoid the chance of a Bad Colocolum rolling into you when it's defeated by a night monster.
Eat a Juicy Peach to ensure you don't get one-shot by a rolling Bad Colocolum if you're worried.
Wicked Trowelies appear on 6F, but shouldn't be an issue if you keep Preservation Pots full.

Tower Area 2 (7-9F)

The final stretch. Special blue doors appear on these floors, which lock you in a room until all monsters are defeated.
It's very dangerous if you get locked in a large room with Sky Dragons, so use a Zen Pot if that happens.

Nashagga (7-9F), Sky Dragon (8-9F), and Steelhead (8-9F) all have high attack power.
Nashagga in particular is extremely dangerous, since it can attack twice in 1 turn - avoid getting hit at all costs.
Use arrows to lower its HP before it's adjacent, and use a staff or scroll if it's next to you. (Talismans can miss)
Sky Dragons and Steelheads are weaker than in Shiren 4, but they still hit hard, so keep an eye on your remaining HP.

Night is full of very annoying monsters.
Dark Scarrabit can attract you and other monsters in the room, Dark Chucker can throw limit broken monsters at you,
and Vile Pumpanshee and Sinister Porkon will ruin your plans to wait for daytime in hallways.
It's hard to predict what will happen at night, so it's recommended to eat a Juicy Peach if you have one.
If you don't have a Monster Detector, don't hesitate to use Navigation Scrolls.

Boss

Boss: Reeva Statue (1200 HP, 75 attack, 20 defense)
※ Gr. Reeva Statue reflects projectiles and thrown items.
※ All of your equipment will become unequipped when the fight begins.
Moves:
・Direct attack - high power
・Shockwave - 15 damage + knockback
・Prophecy:
　・Tidal Wave - Shiren and allies move to a random position, equipment rusts, and scrolls become wet. (DS version only)
　・Lightning - High power attack that deals damage to every creature in the room except Gr. Reeva Statue.
　・Earthquake - All of your equipment becomes unequipped.
　・Gust - Deals knockback, and 5 damage if you collide with a wall.
　・Stray Missile - A cannonball similar to the ones fired by Pop Tanks flies at you, dealing 40 damage.
　・Monsters - Summons multiple Sky Dragons. (If the Dragon family is extinct, summons Trillmans instead)

Many status conditions work on Gr. Reeva Statue (excluding select ones like Cloned), but it'll cure the status after 1 turn.
Use Swift Grass, Power Up Grass, Hard Peach, Invincible Grass to boost Shiren (Jirokichi often collapses right away),
and loop inflicting a status condition (staves or scrolls) → direct attack to have a one-sided fight.
Place an Oil Scroll on the ground and swing a Swap Staff to inflict Tottering status, which lets you deal double damage.

Status inflicting items that stop Gr. Reeva Statue from using special attacks include Seal Staff, Confusion Scroll, and Fear Scroll.
Slow Staff can be used to increase the number of turns you have before it cures the status condition.
Paralysis or Transient staves can be used to paralyze Sky Dragons, and talismans are effective against them too.
Lastly, you'll be able to continue the story even if Jirokichi collapses during the boss fight, so don't worry about reviving him.
