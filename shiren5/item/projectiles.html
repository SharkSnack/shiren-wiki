Wood Arrow

Deals damage to a target from up to 10 tiles away in a straight line.
Low attack power, and it's the most common type of arrow in the game.
Use them to practice your aim, insert single arrows into Presto Pots,
or check for monsters in hallways, etc.

Iron Arrow

Deals damage to a target from up to 10 tiles away in a straight line.
High attack power, and hits harder than a direct attack with a Katana.
Easy to collect from Cross Carts using a Dodger Pot, and Iron Arrow Traps
are about as common as Wood Arrow Traps in this game.

Synthesize with a weapon to create the Anti-Metal rune.

Low attack power, but the arrow pierces through walls and creatures.
Plays well with Monster Detector, but be careful not to hit Shopkeeps.

Poison Arrow

Inflicts Weakened status on a target from up to 10 tiles away in a straight line.
One arrow reduces their attack by 50%, making it effective against power monsters.
Diminishing returns after the first arrow.

However, Poison Arrows grant Buffed status to Mutaikons and Scorpions.

Knockback Arrow

Deals damage from up to 10 tiles away, and occasionally knocks the target back.
The knockback effect is extremely powerful, and lets you defeat enemies without risk.
It's best to collect Knockback Arrows from a Strong Cart using a Dodger Pot.
You can never have enough of these arrows, and a set of 99 Knockback Arrows
can be sold for a little over 5000G if you're low on Gitan.

Truestrike Arrow

Deals damage to a target from up to 10 tiles away in a straight line, and never misses.
The arrow always hits its target, even if you have an Inacc. Bracelet equipped,
a Dodger Pot is in effect, or a Bored Kappa throws the arrow back at you.

Projectiles normally have 84% accuracy, so having a reliable option is nice.
But these arrows are rare, so it's usually better to use inventory space
on sets of wood, iron, poison, or knockback arrows.
