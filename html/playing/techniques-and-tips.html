Readiness

Expect the Worst

The mantra of a rougelike is "Hope for the Best, Expect the Worst".
Generally, the more you plan for negative situations, the further you'll be able to progress.

Example thoughts:

  • What if I don't find food from this point on?
  • Even stronger monsters might appear after this floor.
  • Is my equipment good enough?
  • I see the stairs, but there might be a hidden trap on the way to it.
  • I might be underleveled to advance to the next floor.

There's really an endless list of thoughts or worries that can cross your mind, so it's similar to driving,
where you prepare for and are ready to react to situations. (example: a squirrel running across the road).
It's important to be aware of what has the potential to occur at any given time, and act accordingly.

That said, you don't want to overdo the thinking to the point of getting stuck in decision paralysis.
Overthinking can lead to mistakes as well, so don't stress or worry too much, and enjoy the gameplay.

The key to the main story is to recognize when you're reaching your limit, and escape as needed to regroup.
No one will give you a hard time for escaping, even if it's on a early floor.

Have a Backup For Missed Throws

Projectiles and thrown items have a roughly 84% chance to hit the target.
The chance for 3 projectiles or thrown items to hit the target in a row is less than 60%.

So it's good to think of follow-ups like leading a monster onto a nearby trap before throwing the item.
If a dangerous monster is adjacent to you, it's best to use a staff or scroll instead of relying on a thrown item.

Move Carefully Near Enemies

Many monsters have troublesome abilities and special attacks, so even one missed step can lead to a collapse at times.
So be careful of your inputs, and don't move without intention when there are enemies around.

Don't Dash in Dangerous Places

Dashing makes it quicker to navigate dungeons.
However, as nice as it is, don't dash in dangerous situations like at night, or on floors where dangerous monsters appear.
Really, it might be best to avoid dashing at all times to prevent accidents.

Use the Scout Command

Seeing what monsters are in a room lets you create an informed plan to either fight or run.
Finding out that there was a DJ Mage or Pop Tank family monster after getting hit
by their special attack will be too late to prevent a loss or collapse.

Use Diagonal Movement

One diagonal movement is equal to 2 orthogonal movements.
It's important to get used to using diagonal movement to conserve fullness, and to limit monster spawns.

Common Missed Inputs

Inserted main equipment into a Presto Pot
Often occurs when you're tired, or nervous in late game floors.
Remember that the cursor moves to the top when you press right while viewing the item you're standing on.
Perhaps it's best to make a habit of selecting the item with R before inserting.
Threw an item at a Shopkeep while identifying
Often occurs when you're naming items while checking a price chart, and usually results in a failed adventure.
Place items away from the Shopkeep, or make a habit to face away before opening your inventory.
Pressed B+L to Scout, but shot an arrow instead
Make a habit to press B before L. (Pressing B alone doesn't end your turn)
However, that will make you dash instead of scout if you miss the L input.
Perhaps it's best to always select the Scout option from the menu, instead of using shortcuts.
※ Use the right analog stick to scout on the PS Vita version.

Food Related

Restoring Fullness

It might seem like food items you find on the ground or buy from shops are the only ways to restore fullness at first,
but there are a number of other ways to either restore fullness or obtain items that restore fullness.

Eating to restore fullness:

  • Onigiri -- 50
  • Large Onigiri -- 100
  • Huge Onigiri -- fully restored
  • Rotten Onigiri -- 30
  • Grilled Onigiri -- 60
  • Special Onigiri -- 30
  • Dog Onigiri -- 50
  • Silver Onigiri -- fully restored
  • Gold Onigiri -- fully restored
  • Hard Peach -- 30
  • Peach -- 50
  • Juicy Peach -- 100
  • Rotten Peach -- 15
  • Any grass item -- 5
  • Squid Sushi Scroll -- 40

Other methods to restore fullness:

  • Read a Fixer Scroll while starving -- fully restored
  • Revive using Revival Grass, Undo Grass, or Wanderer Rescue -- fully restored
  • Get hit by Mutaikon family special attacks -- 5
    ※ Use a Cleansing Bracelet, Anti-Cnf. Bracelet, Alert Bracelet, or Immunity Scroll depending on level.
  • Get hit by Grass Boy family special attacks -- 5
  • Get hit by a peach or grass item thrown by a Kappa Pest, Vexing Kappa, or Kappa Troll -- varies by item
  • Get hit by an attack while having a Bowl Shield or a shield with the Pain Fueled rune equipped -- 1 (6% chance)
  • Stay at an Inn (Hermit's Hermitage or Sparrow's Inn) -- fully restored

Methods to obtain food / increase restoration amount:

  • Find items on the ground
  • Buy items from shops (dungeon or village)
  • Obtain items from a Presto Pot
  • Read an Onigiri Scroll -- Large Onigiri
  • Read a Commend. Letter (or show it to an elite store Shopkeep) -- Large Onigiri
    ※ Your Gitan total is set to 0 afterward.
  • Grill Rotten Onigiri (30 → 60), Onigiri (50 → 60), Dog Onigiri (50 → 60), or Special Onigiri (30 → 60)
    ※ Fullness restoration decreases if you grill Large Onigiri or Huge Onigiri.
  • Throw a Rotten Onigiri or Special Onigiri at a Kid Squid family monster to create a Squid Sushi Scroll (30 → 40)
    ※ Other onigiri items work, but fullness restoration decreases.
  • Trigger a Decay Trap while a monster is standing on the trap. -- Rotten Onigiri
    ※ Works on Floating type monsters too.
  • Splash Water on a monster afflicted by Onigiri status -- Rotten Onigiri
    ※ Mostly used with the Onigiri Bonanza ability.
  • Splash Water on a Hard Peach (30 → 50) or Peach (50 → 100)
    ※ Juicy Peach works, but it'll turn into a Rotten Peach.
  • Advance floors to ripen peaches (See next section)
  • Defeat Kumonigiri family monsters or Nigiri Morphs -- Onigiri
  • Defeat Nigiri Bosses -- Large Onigiri
  • Defeat Nigiri Kings -- Huge Onigiri
  • Defeat Foul Nigiri Morphs, Foul Nigiri Bosses, or Foul Nigiri Kings - Rotten Onigiri
  • Defeat Zalokleft family monsters -- random item drop
  • Get hit by Nigiri Morph or Nigiri King special attacks -- Large Onigiri
  • Get hit by Foul Nigiri Morph, Foul Nigiri Boss, or Foul Nigiri King special attacks -- Rotten Onigiri
  • Get hit by Firepuff or Dragon family special attacks -- grills all onigiri outside of pots
  • Get hit by Explochin or Pop Tank family special attacks -- grills all onigiri outside of pots
  • Step on Explosion or Big Explosion traps -- grills all onigiri outside of pots
  • Throw Dragon Grass at a Kappa Pest, Vexing Kappa, or Kappa Troll -- grills all onigiri outside of pots
  • Place unneeded items (single rocks, single arrows, etc.) on the ground in a room with a Field Knave -- Weeds
  • Talk to Tao when you're starving to buy an Onigiri for 500G

Ripening Peaches

Changing floors ripens peaches outside of pots in the order of Hard Peach → Peach → Juicy Peach → Rotten Peach.
It takes 3 floors to ripen a Hard Peach into a Peach, and 2 floors for the others.

Water also ripens a peach by 1 stage, and floor count isn't reset when a peach ripens due to water.
(example: Peach will become a Juicy Peach next floor, ripen it using water, it'll become a Rotten Peach next floor).
Water sources include Water Pots, walking on water using a Waterwalk Bracelet, and tsunamis in the DS version.
Decay traps will always transform a peach into a Rotten Peach.

Juicy Peaches restore the most fullness, but a regular Peach cures negative status conditions and restores strength,
and also grants the powerful Immune status when blessed, so remember that when managing ripeness using pots.
Rotten Peaches can be thrown at enemies to lower attack and defense.

Eating Priority

If you don't have a pot to store food in, it's best to eat food that restores a lot of fullness first
to reduce the loss from stepping on a Decay Trap.

If you have a pot to store food in, or there's no risk of stepping on a Decay Trap,
it's best to eat food that restores the least amount of fullness first to free up inventory space.

Grilling Onigiri

Get hit by fire or explosion damage to grill all onigiri in your inventory that aren't inside pots.

Zen Pots can be used to grill onigiri without receiving damage from fire or explosions.
The Anti-Blast※ rune will make you unable to grill onigiri using explosions.

Inacc. Bracelet + Decay Trap

Equip an Inacc. Bracelet → lure a monster onto a trap → throw an item or shoot an arrow.
This works on Floating type monsters as well, excluding Flamebird family monsters since their body burns items.
(However, if you inflict the Flamebird with Cloned status, you can trap them)

Use this technique with a Decay Trap to create lots of Rotten Onigiri.
The Rotten Onigiri can either be grilled, or thrown at a Kid Squid monster to make a Squid Sushi Scroll.

Killer Arrow + Decay Trap

Similar to the previous technique, but shoot a Killer Arrow instead of equipping an Inacc. Bracelet.
(Killer Arrows have very low accuracy)

Making Squid Sushi Scrolls

Throw an onigiri at a Kid Squid family monster to transform them into a Squid Sushi Scroll.
Mainly performed using Rotten Onigiri to create safe to eat food.

Monsters

Shoot Arrows in Hallways

Many dungeons have limited field of view in hallways.
If you don't have a Monster Detector, Monsterphobic, Scout Bracelet, or Navigation Scroll, you're at risk of running
into monsters and getting hit by an attack before you're able to react, which can result in a collapse.

So in an effort to avoid that, shoot an arrow or throw an unneeded item into the hallway before entering it.
If the item either hit or missed a monster, go ahead and wait for the monster to enter the room from the hallway.
If there wasn't a monster, it's usually safe to enter the hallway, but you'll want to periodically shoot an arrow
or throw an item while navigating the hallway to ensure a monster isn't approaching.

Monster House Encounter

If you encounter a Monster House, stay calm and use the Scout command to check monsters and escape paths.
After that, look at your inventory items to see what options you have to either conquer or escape the situation.

Monsters Immune to Projectiles

Some monsters are effectively immune to projectiles due to their special abilities.
However, you can still hit them with projectiles if you equip a Can. Arm Bracelet or eat Dracon Grass beforehand.
(That said, be aware that Mixers won't swallow items with piercing properties)

  • Mixer family --- Swallows items and synthesizes them. (Number of items it can swallow varies by level)
  • Sweet Nut family --- Swallows items and multiplies experience points, but can explode. (Item is lost either way)
  • Bored Kappa family --- Throws items back at you. (Item categories it can throw varies by level)
  • Flamebird family --- Its body burns projectiles and thrown items.
  • Grass Boy, Pin Kid families --- Covers itself in grass when a grass item is thrown at it.
  • Froggo family --- Thrown Gitan heals it instead of dealing damage. (Also applies to Gitan with piercing properties)
  • Gyaza family --- Converts damage and effects from items into 2 damage. (Also applies to items with piercing properties)

※ Items will hit sleeping Mixers and Sweet Nuts.
　It's easy to make this mistake, since sleeping Mixers swallowed items in some of the previous games in the series.
※ The number of grass items Grass Boys can cover themselves in is equal to their level.
　(Limit broken Grass Gramps and grass items with piercing properties haven't been tested)
※ Kojirouta's Gitan throw special attack has piercing properties and heals Froggos.

Monster Special Ability Types

Monster special abilities can be categorized into three types. (See Monster Ability Chart for details)

1. Special attack
　Can be shut down by statuses besides Sealed, including Afraid, Confused, etc.
　Examples: Nigiri Morph's breath, Fearabbit's attraction, Dragon's fire breath.

2. Special ability
　Can only be shut down by Sealed status.
　Examples: Mixer swallowing items, Gyaza converting effects into 2 damage, Shagga's multi-attack.

3. Innate characteristic
　Cannot be shut down, even by Sealed status.
　Examples: FO-U's 1 damage conversion, Flamebird burning items or multiplying from explosions,
　Floating types being able to move on void tiles, Aquatic types being able to move on water tiles.

Seedie - Early Game

Seedies were significantly buffed in this game, and are now on par with Shiren 4's Chintalas.
They have the same stats as Colum, and require a hit-and-away strategy when you don't have a shield.
Basically attack once, retreat a few steps until your HP recovers, and then attack again.
They're mostly a non-issue once you reach level 2, or if you have a shield equipped.

Mudkin - Unequip Equipment

Mudkin family monsters can lower upgrade value and erase runes, but are harmless when nothing is equipped.
They can multiply when hit by attacks including arrows, so they're tricky to dispatch even from a distance.
So the best method to deal with them without consuming Seal Staff uses is to unequip your equipment.
You can unequip both weapon and shield in 1 turn by inserting them into a pot using multi-select.
They can still multiply when you punch them bare-handed, but the chance is pretty low, so it's not a big problem.

Swordsmans - What's behind you?

Ally NPCs always dodge flying equipment in Shiren 5, but other monsters can be hit by flying equipment.
So it's best to fight Swordsman family monsters with your back against a wall to minimize risk of losing equipment.

On a related note, disarmed equipment will have piercing properties if you have a Can. Arm Bracelet equipped
or if you've eaten Dracon Grass on the current floor, so be extra careful if either of those cases apply.

Grass Boy - Zigzag Movement

Grass Boy family monsters will throw grass at you from a distance when you're lined up with them.
If you approach them in a straight line, you're bound to get hit by their attack, so use diagonal movement instead.
This basic technique is also used against Boy Cart family monsters, DJ Mage family monsters, Dragons, etc.

Gyadon - Protect your Items

Protect staves and scrolls from Gyadon and Gyairas pecking attacks by inserting them into Preservation Pots.
Gyandora and Gyandoron can peck pots, so use the Anti-Peck rune, or use projectiles or staves from a distance.

Scoopie - Place Pots on the Ground

Scoopie family monsters can throw dirt into pots with free space.
This can quickly turn the tide of your adventure if it happens to valuable pots like Blessing Pot or an incense pot.
So it's best to place your most important pot(s) on the ground before fighting Scoopie family monsters.

Items

Removing Tags

Tags can be removed in shops or by the Sentry in Hermit's Hermitage.
Mudkin family special attacks can remove tags as well, and tags are removed when you sell equipment.

Tag Equipment in Dungeons

You can tag equipment by talking to the Shopkeep inside a dungeon shop, or by reading a Tag Scroll.
Nice option if you find a rare item in a post-game dungeon, but don't think you can clear the dungeon to take it home.

Identify Shop Items

Give an Identify Scroll to the Shopkeep to identify all merchandise, or name the items using a price chart as a reference.
If using the first method, you can sell unidentified items beforehand and steal or purchase them again afterward.

See Identifying for more identification tips.

Throw Staves

Throw a staff with 0 remaining uses to get one last use out of it.
However, some staves like Pinning Staff won't have an effect when thrown at a wall.
Magic bullets from staves always hit their target, but thrown staves can miss like any other item.

Reusable Torches

Throw the torch you have equipped at a Mixer, and defeat the Mixer to reclaim an unused torch.

Fever Pots

See Fever Pot for safe insertion conditions.

Duplicate Using Wet Scrolls

Different scrolls can be used to duplicate items if they're transformed into Wet Scrolls beforehand.
The resulting scrolls will be the same item as the first Wet Scroll you inserted when they dry off.

Example:

  1. Insert a Wet Scroll (Blank) → Wet Scroll (Exorcism) → Wet Scroll (Vacuum Slash).
  2. The pot will shatter and drop 6 Wet Scrolls, which will all be Blank Scrolls when they dry off.

How to create Wet Scrolls:

  • Have a Mixer monster swallow a scroll.
  • Pour water on a scroll using a Water Pot.
  • Walk on water tiles using a Waterwalk Bracelet.
  • Get caught in a tsunami. (DS version only)

Duplicate Using 2 Items

Useful for duplicating rare items like True Knife, Onigiri Shield, Wonder Pick, etc.

  1. Have a Gyandora or Gyandoron peck a Fever Pot.
  2. Use 2 Pot God Scrolls on the Fever Pot [0].
    (Items won't be duplicated with a pot size of 1)

Fastest method is to level up a Gyadon located in Tower of Fortune 2~3F.
However, it can't peck the pot if your shield has the Anti-Peck rune, and its attacks are painful without a shield.
You can temporarily erase Anti-Peck, use Poison Arrows, unequip your shield and use a Klein Pot, etc.

Water Pots

Water Pots may appear to be a useless pot at first, but they were significantly buffed in Shiren 5 and have many uses.
They're also reusable as long as you come across a water source, so they can help conserve other item usage.

Effects:

  • Destroy traps
  • Ripen peaches by 1 stage
  • Transform a scroll into a Wet Scroll
  • Decrease weapon or shield upgrade value by 1
  • Turn monsters with Onigiri status into a Rotten Onigiri
  • Lower the attack power of select monsters by 8 stages
  • Inflict Sealed status to select monsters
  • Inflict both Slow and Sealed to select monsters
  • Restore HP by 15 to select monsters
  • Deal damage equal to 50% of max HP to select monsters
  • Double the special attack usage rate of Mutaikon family monsters

See Water Pot for details about which monsters are affected by these effects.

Dirt in Pots

Dirt thrown into pots by Scoopie family monsters have a set chance to turn into grass items when you change floors,
so don't immediately discard valuable pots if they get filled with dirt.

Using a Pot God Scroll on a pot that has dirt in it will still add 1 to its size, but the expanded slot will be dirt.
The grass items that result from dirt will never be blessed or sealed.

See Dirt for details about what happens when dirt is thrown into each type of pot.

Revival Grass in Pots

Unlike Shiren 4, Revival Grass will activate even when its inside a pot, so you can make use of otherwise negative
pots like Unbreakable Pot by keeping Revival Grass (and Undo Grass) in them instead of discarding them.

Oil Scroll in a Pot

Technique that is particularly useful against bosses, but can be applied to any enemy.

Insert an Oil Scroll into a pot that shatters when thrown, and then throw the pot to inflict Tottering status on the same turn.
However, note that the Oil Scroll won't fall underfoot if there's an item or trap on the tile, and the thrown pot can miss.

See Oil Scroll for details about the item.

Trigger Traps Using Pots

Similar to the above Oil Scroll technique.

Place an item inside a pot that shatters when thrown, and then throw the pot at a monster standing on a trap.
The item will fall where the monster is standing, triggering the trap.

Monsters who appear when a Zalokleft Pot or Monster Pot shatters will trigger traps they land on as well.

Presto / Zalokleft Pots in Villages

Presto and Zalokleft pots have limited item tables in village storage facilities,
so keep that in mind if you happen to forget an Escape Scroll.

  • Nekomaneki Village → Onigiri, Escape Scroll
  • Hermit's Hermitage → Fine Torch, Escape Scroll
  • Sparrow's Inn → Fine Torch, Escape Scroll

Incense Pot Colors

Incense colors might be easy to forget while you're still learning the game.

  • Zen: Blue
  • Dodger: Green
  • Reflection: Purple
  • Perceptive: Yellow

Blessed Extraction Scroll Loop

One of the key techniques of Shiren 5.

Reading a blessed Extraction Scroll will bless all items that are extracted from a pot, and the scroll will be consumed.
So as long as one of the items in the pot is a Blank Scroll or Extraction Scroll, you can create a blessed item loop.

There are many uses for this technique, including rapidly increasing equipment upgrade value or level (explained further down),
blessing items like Pot God Scroll, Fixer Scroll, Expulsion Scroll, Angel Seed, Revival Grass, Undo Grass, etc.

Gen's Turf and Page of Youth offer many opportunities to obtain Blank Scrolls through monster licenses,
so this technique isn't necessarily limited to dungeons that allow carry-in items.
(Create a blessed Extraction Scroll by writing Blessing and Extraction on two Blank Scrolls to get started)

※ Note that you can skip having to bless Extraction Scrolls if you obtain a Blessing Pot.

No Damage Itemphobic

Step on an item, and then directly insert the item into a pot to avoid receiving damage.
You can then safely take out or use the item if you inserted it into a Preservation Pot.

Curses / Seals

Removing Curses / Seals

Methods include:

  • Exorcism Scroll
  • Fate Scroll (weapon)
  • Earth Scroll (shield)
  • Darth Scroll (weapon or shield)
  • Plating Scroll (weapon or shield)
  • Blessing Scroll (change cursed to blessed)
  • Fixer Scroll (equipped weapon, shield, bracelet, and staff)
  • Exorcism Pot
  • Blessing Pot (change cursed to blessed)
  • Fever Pot (duplicated items are never cursed, sealed, or blessed)
  • Curse Breaker (Nekomaneki Village or Wandering NPC)
  • Synthesis (insert or throw cursed / sealed item first)
    ※ Mixer synthesis might not remove the curse or seal, depending on item category priority.

Unequipping Cursed Equipment

Methods include:

  • Exorcism Scroll (removes curse)
  • Fate Scroll (removes curse)
  • Earth Scroll (removes curse)
  • Plating Scroll (removes curse)
  • Blessing Scroll (change cursed to blessed)
  • Fixer Scroll (removes curse from equipped weapon, shield, bracelet, and staff)
  • Curse Scroll (change cursed to sealed)
  • Sale Scroll (item is lost)
  • Onigiri Scroll (item is lost)
  • Nymph Grass
  • Strip Trap
  • Curse Trap (change cursed to sealed, place all other items on the ground first)
  • Curse Girl family special attack (change cursed to sealed, place all other items on the ground first)
  • Swordsman family special attack (item category varies by level)
  • Shady Mudkin family special attack (torches only, unequip shield first)
  • Knave King special attack (item is lost)
  • Nigiri King special attack (item is lost, place all other items on the ground first)
    ※ Nigiri Morph never transforms equipped items into Large Onigiri.
  • Curse Breaker (Nekomaneki Village or Wandering NPC)

※ Use Poison Grass, Poison Arrow, Rotten Peach, Water Pot, etc. when relying on a monster's special attack.
※ Use Perception Grass, Trapper Bracelet, Trap Scroll, Mnster House Scrl, Multiplication Trap, Karakuroids to find a trap.

Sealing Shoddy or Glass Equipment

Shoddy Dirk (30 attack) and Glass Dirk (35 attack) can be sealed to nullify their innate negative abilities.
This can be useful early on before you find decent equipment to start working on.

Cursing for Mojo Bracelet

Equipping a Mojo Bracelet allows you to land critical hits, with the chance being based on the number
of cursed or sealed items in your inventory. (excluding items in pots)

Critical hit rate = 5 * x * y + 10%, where x = number of cursed or sealed items, and y = number of Mojo Bracelets.
(So 18 items to reach 100% with 1 bracelet, or 9 items with 2 bracelets)

Methods to curse or seal items:

  • Curse Scroll
  • Curse Pot
  • Imabikiso (rare grass item, place items you don't want cursed or sealed on the ground first)
  • Curse Trap (place items you don't want cursed or sealed on the ground first)
  • Curse Girl family special attack (place items you don't want cursed or sealed on the ground first)

※ Use Poison Grass, Poison Arrow, Rotten Peach, etc. when relying on a Curse Girl's special attack.
※ Use Perception Grass, Trapper Bracelet, Trap Scroll, Mnster House Scrl, Multiplication Trap, Karakuroids to find a trap.

Synthesis

See Gameplay Basics for an overview of synthesis mechanics.

That page details Mixer synthesis item category priorty, how to remove curses or retain blessings when synthesizing,
and status conditions that prevent Mixers from swallowing items.

Sub Weapon / Shield

If your main weapon or shield doesn't have any open rune slots, go ahead and synthesize runes onto a second
weapon or shield to free up inventory space, and then synthesize that later after your main weapon or shield levels up.

Identify Using Mixers

Items that get synthesized with a weapon or shield by a Mixer are identified on the spot.
(So the next time you come across that item, it'll be identified)

Expand Mixer's Stomach

Throw a Stomach Expander at a Mixer to increase the number of items it can swallow by 1. (or 2 if blessed)
This can be repeated up to a max of the Mixer being able to swallow 8 items.
※ Stomach Expanders must be thrown while the Mixer can still swallow items. (example: Before Mixermon swallows 3 items)
※ The Mixer powers up for every Stomach Expander you throw as well, so be careful when fighting it.

Dungeon Tricks

Warping

Sometimes it's faster to warp out of a bad situation, or to a different location on the floor if the stairs is across the map.
This is especially true when speedrunning, or when you're trying to quickly get through floors during the night.

Methods include:

  • Warp Grass
  • Blink Bracelet
  • Waterwalk Bracelet (unequip while on a water tile)
  • Floating Bracelet (unequip while on a water or void tile)
  • Pinning Staff (jump toward a water or void tile)
  • Staff of Sacrifice (get hit by the log onto a water or void tile)
  • Swap Staff (swap places with a monster on a water or void tile)
  • Land on a water or void tile after getting knocked back by a Yanpii family monster
  • Land on a water or void tile after getting hit by DJ Mage's magic bullet (knockback effect)
  • Get hit by DJ Mage's magic bullet (warp effect)
  • Get hit by MC Mage's magic bullet (transient effect)
  • Swing a Transient Staff at a monster while the floor has Reflection status (best with Chakra Cleanse ability)
  • Whack and Warp (ability)
  • "Surrounded" ability formation
  • Black Hole Pot (not a warp, but advance to the next floor using Pit Traps if the dungeon has descending stairs)

※ Deeper floors have greater risk, so it's best to use items like Swift Grass or Invincible Grass before warping.

Waiting for Day / Night

Certain dungeon floor ranges are organized in a way where it's safer to progress either during the day or at night,
which means you'll need to know how to safely change the time of day to make the most of the monster table.
(Also, the Stairlight ability can only be used at night, which reveals the location of the stairs on the map)

The safest method is to read a Night-Day Scroll, but you likely won't come across that scroll often,
and there are usually better uses for a Blank Scroll, like throwing an Extinction Scroll
at the troublesome monster you're trying to avoid in the first place.

Some methods:

  • Place a Sanctuary Scroll to create a safe space in a dead end room / hallway.
  • Paralyze enemies using a Paralysis Staff or Paralyzing rune to create a safe space in a room / hallway.
    ※ Only on floors where Absorbiphant family monsters don't appear.
  • Place Gitan on the ground and have a Froggo family monster stand on it to block a room entrance / hallway.
    ※ Level 1-3 only.
  • Deal enough damage to an Explochin family monster to make it stop moving to block a room entrace / hallway.
  • Place an item on the ground and have a Bored Kappa family monster stand on it to block a room entrance / hallway.
    ※ Stand outside of their throw range, level 1-3 only. (Lv1: 3 tiles, Lv2: 5 tiles, Lv3: 10 tiles)
  • Use a Knockback Staff to have a Moseal family monster block a room entrance / hallway.
  • Use a Fort. Staff to block a room entrance / hallway.
  • Equip a Wall Clip Bracelet and create a safe space inside a wall using a pickaxe or explosion.
  • Equip a Waterwalk Bracelet and wait in a waterway.
  • Equip a Floating Bracelet and wait in a waterway or over a void tile.
  • Enemies tend to walk straight in Shiren 5, so dig a loop + a branch to the side, and wait inside the branch.
    ※ Doesn't have to be a dug out one, naturally generated terrain works too.

※ Pumphantasm family monsters can approach you from inside walls.
※ Don't try to wait on floors where monsters have long-range or floor-wide attacks.
　(examples: Porgon's rock throw, Archdragon's fire breath, Horrabbit's attraction, Kappa Troll's item throw, etc.)
※ Aquatic type monsters can enter the room from a different spot besides the entrance if there are waterways.

Level Up Allies

Angel Seed Method

By far the fastest way to level up allies to Lv99, and works on 3 allies at a time.

  1. Obtain Angel Seeds from Madam Ateska's fortune.
  2. Duplicate the Angel Seeds using Fever Pots.
  3. Bless the Angel Seeds using the blessed Extraction Scroll loop. (explained earlier on this page)
  4. Equip a True Knife + Anti-Parry Brce. to activate a resonance effect that ensures thrown items always hit their target.
    ※ Can be skipped if you don't mind missing.
  5. Bring Dracon Grass, or equip a Can. Arm Bracelet if you're not using 100% accuracy resonance.
  6. Go to Destiny Trail with the 3 allies you want to level up, and line them up in a hallway.
  7. Eat the Dracon Grass, and throw the blessed Angel Seeds to increase ally levels by 6 per throw until Lv99.

※ Koharu levels up through equipment level, rather than experience points.
※ Glorious Staff and Glorious Talisman only grant 500 experience points.

See Rescue Passwords to obtain Fever Pots.

Level Up Monsters

It can be beneficial to intentionally level up a monster to farm specific items or obtain lots of experience points early on.
It's important to weigh the risks and consider the items you have on hand before going for it.

If Shiren's actions level up a Swift monster, the monster can still move or attack 1 time on the same turn.
(example: swinging a Glorious Staff)
If a Swift monster's actions cause it to level up, it won't be able to move or attack after it levels up.
(example: defeating a different monster due to Berserk status)

Some level up methods:

  • Swing or throw a Glorious Staff.
  • Throw a Glorious Talisman, Cheery Grass, or Angel Seed.
  • Swing a Clone Staff and have another monster defeat the monster with Cloned status.
  • Throw a Berserker Tal. or Rage Grass and have the Berserk status monster defeat another monster.
  • Throw a Fear Talisman or read a Fear Scroll and have the Afraid status monster defeat another monster.
  • Throw a Conf. Talisman, read a Confusion Scroll, throw Confusion Grass, or trigger a Spin Trap
    and have the Confused status monster defeat another monster.
  • Throw Blinding Grass or trigger a Blind trap and have the Blind status monster defeat another monster.
  • Have a monster stand between you and the target monster so the target monster's attacks hit the monster.
    ※ Pop Tank family monsters won't fire cannonballs if the explosion won't hit you, even if you're lined up.
    (examples: Boy Cart's arrows, Yanpii's tackle, Dragon's fire breath, Moseal's tackle)
  • Wait for a monster to use their special attack so that another monster takes damage.
    (examples: Tiger Tosser's throw, Foly's lightning)
  • Have a monster defeat an ally character.
  • Be revived by the Boy's Sister NPC.

Notable Targets:

  • Mamel family
    • Cave Mamel → 888 exp, Gitan Mamel → 5500 exp.
    • Level it up into a Cave Mamel or Gitan Mamel, and then defeat it using fixed damage.
      Inflict Sealed status, and then defeat it using direct attacks for lots of equipment skill points.
      ※ Use items like Fear Talisman, Inacc. Talisman, Sanctuary Scroll for safety if defeating it using direct attacks.
    • If you only have 1 level up item, have a Pit Mamel defeat a Colum so that the Colum rolls and defeats another monster,
      resulting in the Pit Mamel leveling up twice and turning into a Gitan Mamel.
  • Grass Boy family
    • Level it up into a Grass Guy or Grass Gramps so that it generates grass items on its own,
      and then farm status up effects (max strength, max HP, max fullness) by repeatedly waiting for it to throw grass.
      ※ It can throw negative grasses like Unlucky Seed and Rage Grass in some dungeons, so check the item table first.
  • Nigiri Baby family
    • Nigiri King → 3750 exp.
    • Instantly defeat it by throwing an onigiri item.
      Nigiri Morph can be used to farm Large Onigiri.
  • Froggo family
    • Froggon → 850 exp.
    • The amount of Gitan it drops increases when it levels up.
      Useful for collecting Gitan for shops, or storing a large sum of Gitan as a projectile.
  • Kid Squid family
    • King Squid → 1300 exp, Squidperor → 2200 exp.
    • Instantly defeat it by throwing an onigiri item.
  • Boy Cart family
    • Collect Iron Arrows and Knockback Arrows.
      Best if you also have a Dodger Pot.
  • Pop Tank family
    • Ornery Tank → 1600 exp, Cranky Tank → 3200 exp.
    • Zen Pot makes you invulnerable to its attacks.
      Can also create a limit broken Cranky Tank for lots of experience points if you have the Anti-Blast※ rune.
    • Pop Tank can also be leveled down into a Pierce Cart for 1100 experience points,
      or into a Cross Cart / Strong Cart to collect arrows using a Dodger Pot.
  • Zapdon family
    • Bunchukdon → 1280 exp, Zotdon → 2222 exp.
    • Innate Slow status, but has high attack and a lightning counter attack.
      It also has high defense, so you might not be able to damage it at low levels or with a weak weapon.
  • Moseal family
    • Momomomoseal → 670 exp.
    • It won't attack you unless you're next to it, and it's best to hit it when it has Apathetic status after tackling.
      However, it doesn't offer many experience points compared to other targets, so it might not be worth the items.
  • Mudkin family
    • Mudder → 950 exp.
    • Completely harmless if you unequip your weapon and shield.
    • Hit it with weak attacks to have it multiply - best if it's standing on water so it heals HP each turn.
      You can also equip an Inacc. Bracelet and trigger weak fixed damage traps to have it multiply.
      ※ Things can get out of hand if you create too many copies of the monster.
  • FO-Uβ family
    • FO-UZ → 888 exp, FO-UZZ → 5555 exp.
    • The items it drops is based on level, so it's usually best to aim for FO-UZZ's Revival Grass drops.
      It won't warp away if you inflict Sealed status, but it has high attack so be careful.

Collecting Arrows

There are a few different methods you can use to collect wood, iron, knockback, or poison arrows.
Aim for Strong Cart to collect powerful knockback arrows if you use either of the first two Boy Cart family methods.

Use Boy Cart family monsters

  • Boy Cart family + Dodger Pot
    • Dodger Pot makes all projectiles miss, so simply collect all of the arrows that land on the ground.
      However, arrows that miss can trigger traps when they land on them, so don't forget to check for traps beforehand.
    • Use items like Slumber Scroll, Swift Foe Scroll, Swift Staff, Swift Talisman, Swift Grass for increased efficiency.
  • Boy Cart family + Bored Kappa
    • Position the Bored Kappa so it's between Shiren and the Boy Cart family monster,
      and then collect all of the arrows the Bored Kappa catches and throws at Shiren.
    • Don't apply Swift status to the Bored Kappa.
  • Boy Cart + Strong shield
    • Equip a strong shield and step in place to get hit by Boy Cart's arrows, and collect all of the arrows that miss.
      If your HP gets low, enter a hallway to regenerate HP.
    • Shiren takes 1~4 damage with Shoddy Plank (30 def), and 1 damage with Glass Buckler (35 def),
      so you can use either shield to farm arrows by intentionally sealing it beforehand.
      It can also be done using Lv8 Targite (25 def), Red Shield (27 def), or Fuuma Shield (30 def) + some upgrade value.
      Hard Peach, Rotten Peach, Poison Arrow, Poison Grass, or Super status's defense up can make up for lower defense.
    • Usually end up with about 1 stack of arrows if performed until the wind blows, but doing so depletes a lot of fullness.
    • Can be performed on any floor where Boy Cart or Pop Tank family monsters appear if you use an item like Unlucky Staff.

Trigger arrow traps with thrown items

  • Wood, Iron, or Poison arrow traps
    • If the trap isn't against a wall, you can throw a Rock or Porky Rock, or throw an item from 10 tiles away.
      Pickaxe can be used to create enough distance, and Fort. Staff can be used to create a wall next to the trap.

Step on arrow traps

  • Notes about stepping on arrow traps
    • Arrow traps have 100% accuracy, and stepping on traps can cause you to lose Super or Awakened-ish status,
      so you'll generally want to use the thrown item method instead of any of these, unless you don't have items
      or don't want to waste valuable Fort. Staff uses.
  • Dodger Pot
    • Dodger Pot causes even arrow traps to miss, so simply dodge the arrows and pick them up.
      Missed arrows won't land on the trap underfoot, so the trap can only be triggered one time per turn.
      Therefore similar to Fort. Staff, it's quite wasteful to use a Dodger Pot for Wood or Iron arrows,
      as opposed to saving it for higher level Scoopie, Bored Kappa, Mutaikon, or Porky family monsters,
      or collecting Knockback Arrows using a Strong Cart.
  • Mixer
    • Step on the trap so that Mixer swallows the arrows that fly at you, and then defeat the Mixer.
      Extremely inefficient, so consider it more of a bonus than a realistic method to collect arrows.
      That said, it could be useful in Bizarre Tower on floors where Mixers have very high spawn rates.
  • Bored Kappa
    • Place an item 3 or more tiles away so that the Bored Kappa stands on it without throwing it,
      and then step on the trap so that the Bored Kappa catches the arrow and throws it toward you.
      If you're too close, the thrown arrow will land on the trap underfoot and cause you to get hit by an arrow.
  • Iron Door
    • Turn so that you're facing to the left of the door, and collect the arrows that hit the door.
      Note that it won't work with spear gates, since projectiles can pass through them.
      Pretty inefficient, since you frequently have to stop to collect arrows before they start vanishing.

Making Gitan

  • Hunt Monsters
    • Defeat Froggo family monsters or Gitan Mamels like crazy.
  • Use Shops
    • If the store has a Presto Pot for sale, insert single arrows and sell the transformed items inside the pot.
      You'll usually make a profit even after having to pay for the difference in pot size.
    • Sell all of your items, and then either steal or read a Coupon Scroll to get them back.

Nigiri Morph Factory

The idea is to use a Nigiri Morph (55 HP) to intentionally transform inventory items into Large Onigiri.
Many players feel like it's too much of a chore, but there's no denying how effective it is for alleviating hunger issues.
If you don't mind the routine, it's good to go for it when you happen to have the required items on hand.

Items

Required:

  • Items to be transformed into onigiri
    • Single arrows or rocks, or other unneeded items.
  • Level up item
    • You'll need an item to level up a Nigiri Baby into a Nigiri Morph.
      Easiest items are Cheery Grass, Glorious Staff, Glorious Talisman.
      Other items include Clone Staff, Rage Grass, Fear Scroll, etc. (See Level Up Monsters for more options)

Nice to Have:

  • Poison Arrow, Poison Grass
    • You'll receive quite a few hits, so it's important to reduce the amount of damage you take.
      Poison Grass is the better option since it deals less damage and inflicts Slow status.
  • Rotten Peach
    • Used to lower Nigiri Morph's attack.
      If used with Poison Arrows, throw the Rotten Peach after shooting the arrows since the peach also lowers defense.
  • Hard Peach
    • Increases your defense.
  • Strong shield
    • Basically required if you don't have items to lower the Nigiri Morph's attack or increase your defense.
      Shields like Red Shield or Fuuma Shield are good options, or a sealed Shoddy Plank or Glass Buckler.
      ※ Don't use a shield that has the Retribution rune.
  • Slow Staff
    • Decreases efficiency, but it can be used to help you safely regenerate HP between hits.
  • HP recovery items (Herb, Otogiriso, etc.)
    • Nigiri Morphs usually can't survive 3 Poison Arrow hits, so throwing a healing item lets you shoot more Poison Arrows.

Steps

  1. Check the room for traps.
    Any trap can cause problems, but you especially don't want to step on a Pit Trap or Monster Trap.

  2. Prepare items on the ground.
    After checking for traps, shoot arrows or throw rocks at a wall to separate them into single items.
    If you want to increase max fullness from 100 → 200, you'll need to eat 25 Large Onigiri when you're full. (26 total)
    It's best to stock up on a couple of extra Large Onigiri for the road too, so aim for 28 items or so.
    Of course, feel free to adjust the quantity based on other inventory items or play style.

  3. Trade items to add them to inventory.
    Hold B and move onto an item to step on it without picking it up, and trade the item with an inventory item.
    This lets you pick up items like single arrows or rocks without them automatically getting grouped together.
    ※ Don't sort your inventory after doing this, or else all single arrows and rocks will get grouped together.

    Inventory should be equipment, Poison Arrows or Poison Grass, and items to be turned into onigiri.
    ※ Your equipped weapon, shield, and bracelets will never be transformed into an onigiri by a Nigiri Morph,
    　but set arrows, rocks, staves, and talismans can be transformed, so don't carry anything you want to keep.

    It's sometimes better to place the rest of your items in a different location, depending on the monster table.
    (example: Place items in the stairs room on 22F of Onigiri Hollow, since Grass Bros can throw Warp Grass)
    ※ Remember where you place important items in case you need to use them in an emergency.

  4. Have Nigiri Morph transform inventory items.
    Once the above preparations are complete, it's time to transform items into Large Onigiri.
    If you're receiving too much damage, don't hesitate to use Poison Arrows, Poison Grass, Slow Staff, etc.
    Poison (Weakened status) in particular has a drastic effect, lowering damage dealt by 50% at stage 1.
    Step in place if you can tank the hits, or circle around the room to regenerate HP as you get hit by its attacks.
    ※ Be careful not to pick up your other items while circling around the room.

    Trade the onigiri with other single arrows or rocks, or place / throw the onigiri on the ground during the process.
    Once you've obtained the number of onirigi you were aiming for, defeat the Nigiri Morph to finish up.

Tips / Cautions

  • Leveling up a Nigiri Baby
    This is a non-issue if you use items like Cheery Grass, Glorious Staff, or Glorious Talisman.

    However, when you don't have such items, you can use the current floor's monster table to your advantage.
    Froggo and Moseal commonly appear alongside Nigiri Baby, and both are easy targets to use as level up fodder.
    Place Gitan on the ground to control Froggo's position, and Moseal has high HP, so it's good to damage it a bit first.
    Moseal is better for Cloned status, and Froggo is better for Afraid and Berserk statuses.

  • Using a Poison Arrow Trap
    Shooting Poison Arrows to lower Nigiri Morph's attack is standard practice, but they can deal too much damage
    if you've leveled up, have increased your max strength, or have boosted attack from Super status.
    So in that case, use Poison Arrows from a Poison Arrow Trap instead, which only deals about 5 damage per hit.
    ※ The downside is that the trap might break after 1 use, and you might lose Super or Awakened-ish status.

  • Remember item locations
    This was stated earlier, but it's worth repeating - remember where you place your items in case of an emergency.
    All of the preparation means nothing if you collapse from not having a way to deal with a different monster
    that entered the room while you were busy preparing.

  • Beware of Tiger Tosser
    Tiger Tossers appear alongside Nigiri Baby in some dungeons, and can easily ruin your plans by throwing
    a weakened Nigiri Morph and leveling up, so be especially careful if you've used multiple Poison Arrows.
    It's best to memorize that Nigiri Morph has 55 HP, and that Tiger Tosser's throw deals 5 damage.

    One way to prevent accidents is to block the room entrance using items like Fort. Staff or Paralysis Staff.
    Froggos can also be used to block entrances - simply place Gitan down where you want the Froggo to stay.

  • Beware of Zalokleft, Grass Boy, Absorbiphant
    Nigiri Morph appears alongside Green Zalokleft, Grass Bro, and Absorbiphant in many dungeons.
    Don't let Zaloklefts steal items you've placed on the ground, and keep grass items inside pots.
    Try to position yourself in a way that prevents an Absorbiphant from curing Nigiri Morph's status conditions.

Zalokleft Hunting

Simply defeat Zaloklefts to obtain items.
It's best to have the Anti-Theft rune or the floor-wide effect from a Perceptive Pot to avoid getting your items stolen.
It's probably not worth keeping a Perceptive Pot in your inventory for this purpose, since it has few other uses.

Zalokleft drops have their own item table, and generally don't include anything too special.

Grass Boy / Bro Hunting

Collect grass items by defeating Grass Boy or Grass Bro monsters.
Excellent for obtaining grasses for Mixer synthesis, and to identify grasses early on.
It's best to hunt Grass Boys since Mixer usually appears soon after, but hunger can be an issue.

Grass Guy / Gramps Factory

Pin Poppa and Pin Gramps have the ability to generate their own grass items,
so the idea is to have them throw or spread the grass they generate at Shiren to increase his stats.

The point of this technique is to gain permanent stat increases, so that means you're aiming
for Strength Grass, Stomach Expander, Herb, Otogiriso, Heal Grass, and Life Grass.
However, HP regeneration slows down with higher max HP, and Nigiri Morph factory is much more
efficient at increasing max fullness, so this technique is mainly used to increase max strength.

It's recommended to only bother with this in dungeons where Strength Grass is common,
or in dungeons where there aren't many negative grass items in the item table.
(Pitfall of Life, Underground Manor General District, Monster Hotspot, and possibly Onigiri Hollow)
And to reinforce this point - it's not worth the effort to perform this technique in other dungeons.

Items

  • Immunity Scroll, blessed Peach
    • Protects you from grasses that inflict status conditions, including Confusion, Sleepy, and Rage grass.
      Skip this in dungeons where negative effect grasses aren't in the item table, like Pitfall of Life and Underground Manor.
  • Staunch Bracelet, Cleansing Bracelet
    • Protects you from effects that Immunity Scroll can't block, such as Stomach Shrinker lowering max fullness.
      Immunity Scroll can prevent Slow status from Poison Grass, but it can't block the strength loss.
      So Cleansing Bracelet is needed in Onigiri Hollow, but can be skipped in Pitfall of Life and Underground Manor.
  • Paralysis Staff, Hide Pot, Transient Staff
    • Used for method 2.
      Paralysis Staff is likely the most consistent item.
  • Fort. Staff
    • Used for method 3, but isn't in the item table for Pitfall of Life or Underground Manor.
      Method 3-2 is likely better for Onigiri Hollow.
  • Pickaxe
    • Used for method 3.
  • Steady Shield
    • Protects against Warp Grass, but it's usually fine even if you don't have it.
      Just be sure to perform this technique in the stairs room in Onigiri Hollow to avoid getting stuck without this shield.
  • Level up item
    • Items like Glorious Staff or Glorious Talisman.
      Required if leveling up a Grass Boy or Grass Bro early on. (See Level Up Monsters for other options)
      Grass Dude and Grass Gramps have high attack, so be careful not to stand adjacent to them.
  • Reflection Pot
    • DJ Mage family monsters often appear alongside Grass Boy family monsters,
      so use this pot to protect against magic bullet effects like knockback, warp, transient, etc.
      ※ Higher priority if you don't have an Immunity Scroll or blessed Peach.
  • Swap Staff
    • Used for method 3-2.
      Also useful for method 2 when positioning a non-Pin Kid family monster in the corner of a room.

Checking for Stairs? Trap

Select dungeons generate a trap called Stairs? Trap near the end, which look like stairs but actually hide a random trap.
Performing an attack over the Stairs? Trap tile won't reveal it, and stepping on it makes the stairs vanish and triggers the trap.
It can't be detected by thrown rocks either, so it can be quite troublesome to deal with.

That said, there are a few things you can do to determine if the stairs is real.

  • Destroy the Stairs? Trap

    If you stop on a tile that is diagonal from the stairs, it's real.

Remove Enemy Auras

Talismans have the added ability to remove enemy auras, so you can simply throw a talisman.
Note that the aura removal occurs after the talisman's effect.
(example: Swift Talisman at a yellow aura enemy: speed increases → yellow aura is removed, speed resets to normal)

Other options include Seal Staff, Curse Trap, and the Mine! Mine! Mine! and Room Sealant abilities.

Lag Check

Perform a direct attack, and then change the direction you're facing.
If an enemy is nearby, there will be a bit of lag before the direction change.
It's a powerful technique once you get used to it, since it can be used in hallways or at night where visibility is limited.
However, keep in mind that it costs a turn to check each time, so you might want to avoid using it on floors with magma,
and on floors where monsters have long-range or floor-wide attacks.

Earning / Collecting

Points

Destiny Trail Trap Bracelets

  1. Obtain 2 Trap Bracelets. (See Rescue Passwords, or search in Inori Cave)
  2. Equip a weapon and shield set that grants 2 bracelet resonance, and equip the 2 Trap Bracelets.
    ※ Don't forget an Escape Scroll.
  3. Go to Destiny Trail and collect points until the 3rd wind. (1-2F)
  4. Read an Escape Scroll, and repeat as needed.

Earns about 4,000 points per run. (~20 Blank Scrolls)
For efficiency, bring Koharu and step in place until fullness depletes by 15 before stepping on traps. (Using CR Diet rune)

Trap Scrolls in Destiny Trail

  1. Obtain a Blessing Pot, and be able to write Trap on Blank Scrolls. (See Rescue Passwords)
  2. Bless the 5 Trap Scrolls using the Blessing Pot and an Extraction Scroll, and prepare many pots with Extraction Scrolls.
    ※ Don't forget an Escape Scroll.
  3. Go to Destiny Trail and read 5 Trap Scrolls → collect points → bless the 5 Trap Scrolls, repeat until 3rd wind. (1-2F)
  4. Read an Escape Scroll, and repeat as needed.

Earns about 15,000 points per run.

Trap Scrolls in Gorger's Manor

Gorger's Manor offers 200 points per Point Switch near the end. (48-50F)

Similar to the above method, but loop 4 Trap Scrolls and 1 Trap Deletion Scrl instead.
(Trap Deletion Scrl erases all traps except for Point Switches)

If you don't want to risk carrying a Blessing Pot, prepare blessed Extraction Scrolls instead.

Blank Scrolls

Monster Licenses → Blank Scrolls

Far inferior method compared to exchanging points, but doesn't require any preparations.

  1. Be able to write Escape on Blank Scrolls.
  2. Go to Gen's Turf and collect lots of monster licenses, and escape after one turns into a Blank Scroll.
  3. Once you've collected many Mamel, Seedie, and Chintala licenses, go to Destiny Trail to turn them into Blank Scrolls.

    (See Tanuki Passwords for Gambler's and Blessing scrolls, and Rescue Passwords for Extraction Scroll)
  2. Prepare many pots filled with Gambler's Scrolls, and a pot with 5 Extraction Scrolls inside.
    (Blank Scrolls = 200 points, use a point collection technique)
  3. Exchange for 2 more Blank Scrolls, and prepare a blessed Extraction Scroll.
  4. Use the blessed Extraction Scroll on the 5 Extraction Scrolls pot.
  5. Use the blessed Extraction Scrolls on the Gambler's Scroll pots.
    (Save one blessed Extraction Scroll to bless another set of 5 Extraction Scrolls for efficiency)
  6. Fill your inventory with weapons and shields you want to level up, and go to Destiny Trail 5F.
    ※ Don't forget to bring an Undo Grass / Escape Scroll.
  7. Read the Gambler's Scrolls, aiming for the effect that increases the level of all equipment in your inventory by 1.
    ※ Equipment in pots won't level up.

※ Use a Blessing Pot to eliminate the need to bless Extraction Scrolls if desired.

Notes:
・This technique can be used to level up Koharu.
・Don't bring any Gitan with you, since the bankruptcy effect reduces your Gitan total to 0.
・The reason you do this on 5F is so that you don't change floors when the floor warp effect occurs.
・Don't leave important items on the ground when you read Gambler's Scroll, since the explosion effect can destroy them.
・Bring Undo Grass instead of an Escape Scroll, since the explosion effect can cause accidents. (or use Anti-Blast rune)

Gitan

Coupon Scrolls

  1. Be able to write Coupon on Blank Scrolls. (See Tanuki Passwords)
  2. Stock up on Coupon Scrolls. (Blank Scrolls = 200 points, bless them for efficiency)
  3. Insert high-value items into Preservation Pots, and search for shops in Tower of the Past / Tower of the Present.
    ※ Don't forget an Escape Scroll.
  4. Sell all of your high value items → read Coupon Scroll → repeat until Gitan total reaches 999,999.

Repeat 100 times to max out your bank, which rewards you with a Fuuma Sword. (Plus a trophy for PS Vita version)

Catstones

Before Entering Post-Game Dungeons

The Tanuki who sells Catstones will only go to Destiny Trail, Tower of the Past, Tower of the Present,
Tower of the Future, and Tower of Fortune, so you can easily collect Catstones using the Sparrow Transport.

After Entering Post-Game Dungeons

Collect Catstones by cheating at Pick-A-Choice shops in Lost Well. (Read Collection Scrolls)

Soft-Resetting for Location

Quit the game without saving to change the Tanuki's location when it changes after purchasing a Catstone.
※ Save once after depositing all items and Gitan before using this method. (Point Card will be set to 0 as well)

Experience Points

Reading Gambler's Scroll makes it trivial to reach level 99.
However, maxing experience points requires over double the amount of experience points needed to reach level 99.
(About 1,000 Abyssal Dragons worth)

Monster Summoner

Simply equip 2 Monster Summoner bracelets and go through a 99F dungeon defeating higher level monsters.

Inori Cave Porgon + Blazebird

This requires a long setup, so it has its own page. (Achieves 9,999 monsters defeated on 1 floor at the same time)

99 Use Staves

New Item Bracelets

Unlike Shiren 4, Upgrade Pots have a random chance of shattering when changing floors in Shiren 5,
so use the "Switching floors may upgrade weapons, shields, staves, or Gitan" new item bracelet effect instead.
(It selects 1 item at random when you change floors and increases upgrade value or use count by 1, excluding pots)

Required:

  1. Two new item bracelets with the "Switching floors may upgrade weapons, shields, staves, or Gitan" effect.
  2. New item staves with the Transient → Swap effect. (Ideally, enough staves to last 99 floors)
  3. Weapon and shield set that grants 2 bracelet resonance.
  4. An ally NPC. (Max level Koharu is best for consistency)

Steps:

  1. Head to Inori Cave, and insert all items except for the above equipment and staves into pots.
  2. Equip the weapon and shield set, and both new item bracelets.
  3. Set the new item staff to the L button, and swing it at your ally to warp to the stairs → repeat 99 floors. (~10 minutes)
    (This increases upgrade value or use count by 99 x 2 = 198 per run, since there are 2 bracelets)
  4. Repeat until you have a nice stock of 99 use new item staves with the Transient → Swap effect.
  5. Repeat the process, this time carrying other staves.

NPCs Dodge Equipment

NPCs always dodge weapons, shields, and bracelets.
It's possible to use this to your advantage when fighting Swordsman family monsters.

Set Projectiles / Staves

You can set projectiles and staves, letting you use the item by pressing the L button.
Setting an item doesn't end your turn, and the item won't be unequipped by Strip Traps.

Selling Sale Pots in Villages

Gitan inside Sale Pots will be included in the selling price.

Reclaim Equipment Without Tags

An untagged weapon or shield you lost can occasionally be found in the field south of the Beginner House in Inori Village.

Night Monster Movement

All monsters in Shiren 5 have wandering movement at night. (This wasn't the case in Shiren 4)

Night Monster Experience Points

Most night monsters offer less experience points compared to their daytime counterparts.
However, Sweet Nut, Moseal, and Myriman family monsters offer more experience points at night.

Monster Licenses at Night

Night monsters won't become friendly.

Monster House BGM Change

The music returns to normal when the room empties, instead of when all monsters are defeated like Shiren 4.

N'mach and N'dup in Villages

N'machs and N'dups are automatically identified when you enter a village. (This wasn't the case in Shiren 4)
Deposit them using multi-select (R button) to erase them.

New Items Warning

You can't unregister new items once registered, and you can only register up to 64 items.
If you want to experience post-game dungeons without breaking the balance, never register new items.

Can't Register Monsters While Blind

Monsters you defeat while you're afflicted with the Blind status condition won't be registered to the Monster Book.
This is the case even if you have Monster Detector or Scout Bracelet equipped.
